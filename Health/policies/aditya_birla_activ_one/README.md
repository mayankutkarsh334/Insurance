## Key strengths
- **HealthReturns earn-back — up to 100% of premium back** for staying active; unique, big net-cost cut for a young healthy buyer (M4)
- **Cleanest waiting-period story:** individual can *pay to shorten* the 3-yr PED wait, + optional **Day-1 waiver for 7 chronic conditions** (M2)
- **No room cap, no sub-limits, 90-day pre-hosp** (beats HDFC's 60), modern treatments + mental illness up to SI, SI to ₹6Cr (M1)
- **Age-banded pricing** (gentler increases) + strong Aditya Birla group backing (M4/M5)

## Watch-outs
- **Docs are the NXT variant, not the shortlisted MAX** — consumables (Claim Protect) are a **paid optional** in NXT vs inbuilt in HDFC/MAX (M1)
- **Growth bonus (Super Credit) is optional + capped at 6×** vs HDFC's inbuilt-uncapped (M1)
- **Lower ICR (68% vs HDFC 85%)** — pays out less per premium rupee (M3)
- **Younger insurer (~9 yrs)**, lighter foreign partner, unverified health-entity rating/profitability (M5)
- **HealthReturns lock-in** (forfeited on switching) + no cancellation refund for instalment payers (M6)
