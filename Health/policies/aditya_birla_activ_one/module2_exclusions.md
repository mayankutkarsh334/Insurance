# Module 2 — Exclusions & Waiting Periods

_Source: **binding policy wording** `Activ One` (UIN ADIHLIP27048V022627, `resources/`) — **NXT variant** Product Benefit Table + Section D. Wording governs._

> **Read this first — plain-English framing**
> "Waiting periods" = the time you must hold the policy *before* certain illnesses are covered. "Exclusions" = things never covered. This is where most claim denials happen — so a plan that covers things *sooner* and excludes *less* is safer.

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **PED (pre-existing disease)** | An illness you already had before buying — covered only after a waiting period |
| **Specific-disease waiting** | A 2-year wait for a fixed list (cataract, hernia, knee, etc.) |
| **Co-pay** | A share of every bill *you* must pay — zero is best |
| **Sub-limit** | A cap on a particular treatment — "no sub-limits" means each is covered up to your full pot |
| **Day-1 chronic cover** | Diabetes/BP/etc. covered immediately, no waiting (a rare, valuable extra) |

## SI-scaling read
- Waiting/exclusion rules are **identical across all SI tiers** (₹5L–₹6Cr) — the 3-yr PED / 2-yr specific / no-co-pay / no-sub-limit profile doesn't change with cover size.
- Same enhancement-of-SI reset trap as HDFC — a larger base up front avoids re-triggering waiting on later top-ups.

## Module 2 score: **5 / 5**
Cleaner than the HDFC benchmark on waiting/exclusions: **zero mandatory co-pay, zero sub-limits, modern treatments & mental illness up to SI**, and — crucially — an **individual can pay to shorten the 3-year PED wait to 1–2 years** (HDFC restricts this to the sales channel), plus an **optional Day-1 waiver for 7 chronic conditions**. Default PED is 3 years (same as HDFC), but the customer-accessible reductions and chronic waiver make its waiting-period story the strongest seen so far.
