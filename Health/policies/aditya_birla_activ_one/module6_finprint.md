# Module 6 — Fine Print & Longevity

_Source: **binding policy wording** `Activ One` (UIN ADIHLIP27048V022627, `resources/`), Section E General Conditions + IRDAI norms. The wording helpfully includes plain "Simpler version" boxes._

> **Read this first — plain-English framing**
> This is the contract's small print — the clauses that decide whether the insurer can drop you, deny a late claim, or short-change a refund years from now. For a decades-long policy, these matter as much as the benefits.

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **Lifelong renewability** | They must let you renew for life — can't drop you for ageing or claiming |
| **Moratorium** | After 5 years your cover becomes "unquestionable" (except proven fraud) |
| **Grace period** | Extra days to pay a late renewal without losing continuity |
| **Free-look** | A 30-day "try it and return it" window after buying |
| **Portability** | Right to switch insurer keeping your served waiting periods |
| **Lock-in** | Something that makes leaving costly (here: losing earned HealthReturns) |

## SI-scaling read
- Fine-print rights are **identical across SI tiers**.
- Same enhancement-of-SI trap as HDFC — a larger base up front avoids restarting the moratorium/waiting on later top-ups.

## Override-veto check
- Lifelong renewal ✅ · No claim-based loading ✅ · Withdrawal risk mitigated by guaranteed migration ✅
- The HealthReturns lock-in and instalment-no-refund are **frictions, not dealbreakers**.
- **Override veto: NONE triggered.**

## Module 6 score: **4 / 5**
Solid, standard-compliant small print — **lifelong renewal, no max entry age, 30-day grace/free-look, 60-month moratorium, portability with continuity, no claim-based loading**, and unusually reader-friendly "Simpler version" boxes. Held below HDFC's 5 by two genuine frictions HDFC lacks: the **HealthReturns lock-in** (accrued earn-back is forfeited if you switch — and matters more given this is a young, repricing-prone product) and **no cancellation refund for instalment payers**. **No override veto.**
