> **Read this first — why the company matters**
> You'll keep this policy for decades. So beyond features, ask: **will this insurer stay solvent and pay fairly 20–30 years out?** This module checks Aditya Birla Health's financial strength, backing, and track record.

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **Solvency ratio** | Safety cushion — times over it can cover expected claims. Law needs ≥1.5×; higher = safer |
| **SAHI** | Standalone health insurer — a health-*only* company (more focused than a general insurer that also does car/home) |
| **Promoter / JV partner** | The big owners standing behind it |
| **Credit rating (AAA)** | Independent grade of financial strength; AAA is the top |
| **Book maturity** | How long the insurer has been paying claims — older = more proven |

## SI-scaling read
- Insurer strength is **the same whatever SI tier** you buy — a ₹10L and a ₹1Cr policyholder sit on the same balance sheet.
- The youth/lean-solvency point matters most for **very large, long-dated claims** (high SI, decades out) — where a longer track record and a heavyweight reinsurer (HDFC's edge) are most reassuring.

## Module 5 score: **4 / 5**
A solid, health-focused insurer backed by the **Aditya Birla group** — a top Indian conglomerate — with improving solvency (1.98), huge reach (24M lives) and a clean record. Held below the HDFC benchmark (5) on three real counts: it's **much younger (~9 yrs vs 24)**, its **foreign partner (Momentum) is lighter than Munich Re**, and its **health-entity credit rating and profitability are unverified** (don't credit it the Aditya Birla *Sun Life* AAA). The strong parent brand de-risks the youth, but this is a notch below HDFC's elite institutional strength.
