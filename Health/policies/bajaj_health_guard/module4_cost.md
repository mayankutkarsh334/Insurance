# Module 4 — Cost & Premium

_Source: **binding rate chart** (Health Guard Platinum, UIN BAJHLIP26073V082526, `resources/premium_chart.pdf`) + wording discount/loading clauses (`resources/wording_current.pdf`). All premiums **Zone A, Individual, exclusive of tax**._
_Profile: Individual, age 26, metro tier-1 (**Zone A**) · SI ₹10L / ₹25L / ₹50L / ₹1Cr_

> **Plain-English intro.** This module answers "what will it cost — now and over a lifetime?" Health insurance premium isn't fixed: it jumps as you age (in 5-year **bands** here), and the insurer reprices the whole product for medical inflation. Three things to watch: (1) the **age-curve** (how the premium climbs), (2) how premium **scales with sum insured** (bigger cover is cheaper *per rupee* — a key lever), and (3) the **repricing risk** (the silent long-term cost). Good news up front: **individual health insurance is now GST-free (0%)**, so the quoted base premium is what you actually pay.

---

## 📈 Premium by age — the age-loading curve (Platinum, Zone A, Individual)

Premiums are **age-banded (5-year bands)** — the rate is flat *within* a band and steps up when you cross into the next. Values below are the **base premium = amount payable** (GST is 0% for individual policies since 22 Sep 2025).

| Age (band) | **₹10L** | **₹25L** | **₹50L** | **₹1Cr** |
|-----------|:--------:|:--------:|:--------:|:--------:|
| **26** (26–30) | ₹17,633 | ₹21,110 | ₹26,267 | ₹35,531 |
| **35** (31–35) | ₹18,112 | ₹21,684 | ₹26,982 | ₹37,966 |
| **45** (41–45) | ₹25,999 | ₹31,143 | ₹38,774 | ₹50,862 |
| **55** (51–55) | ₹42,571 | ₹51,018 | ₹63,550 | ₹86,573 |
| **65** (61–65) | ₹66,550 | ₹83,074 | ₹96,617 | ₹1,13,335 |
| **75** (71–75) | ₹1,15,538 | ₹1,42,003 | ₹1,67,638 | ₹1,96,798 |

**How to read the age-curve (₹25L):**
| Decade | Premium change | What's happening |
|--------|----------------|------------------|
| 26 → 35 | ₹21,110 → ₹21,684 (**+2.7%** over 9 yrs) | **Nearly flat** — youngest bands barely differ. Buying young locks a cheap band |
| 35 → 45 | +43.6% | Curve begins to climb |
| 45 → 55 | +63.8% | Steepening |
| 55 → 65 | +62.8% | Steep |
| 65 → 75 | +70.9% | Steepest — old-age cost |

> **Finding 1 — the first decade is almost free of age-loading.** A 26-year-old's premium barely moves until ~35, then the classic health-premium "J-curve" kicks in after 45. This rewards buying **young** (you ride the flat part for years) — but note the age-band jumps are *only half the story* (see the 8% repricing red flag below).

## 💡 How premium scales with SI — bigger cover is far cheaper *per rupee* (age 26, Zone A)

This is the most decision-relevant insight for a young buyer:

| SI | Annual premium | Premium **per ₹1L of cover** | Premium multiple vs ₹10L | Cover multiple vs ₹10L |
|----|:--------------:|:----------------------------:|:------------------------:|:----------------------:|
| ₹10L | ₹17,633 | **₹1,763** | 1.00× | 1× |
| ₹25L | ₹21,110 | **₹844** | 1.20× | 2.5× |
| ₹50L | ₹26,267 | **₹525** | 1.49× | 5× |
| ₹1Cr | ₹35,531 | **₹355** | 2.02× | 10× |

```
Premium per ₹1 lakh of cover (age 26) — economies of scale
 ₹1,763 ██████████████████  ₹10L
   ₹844 █████████           ₹25L   ← more than HALF price per lakh vs ₹10L
   ₹525 █████               ₹50L
   ₹355 ████                ₹1Cr   ← one-fifth the per-lakh cost of ₹10L
```

> **Finding 2 — going from ₹10L to ₹25L buys 2.5× the cover for only +20% premium; ₹10L→₹1Cr is 10× cover for ~2× premium.** Because a big chunk of the premium is a fixed base, **each extra lakh of SI gets cheaper.** Combined with M1 (no room cap at ₹10L+, full Super-Bonus scaling) and M2 (enhancement resets waiting → buy high SI *upfront*), the clear takeaway: **a 26-year-old should buy a high SI now** (₹25L–₹50L is the value sweet-spot), not a small one to "upgrade later."
> At older ages this compresses even more — at 75, ₹1Cr costs only ~1.7× the ₹10L premium for 10× the cover. High SI is disproportionately cheap.

## Premium dynamics (levers, discounts, taxes)

| Lever | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Claims-experience discount (premium ↑/↓ with claims)** | ✅ **Asymmetric & buyer-friendly:** wording bars any renewal loading based on *individual* claim experience, **"however, discount in premium may be provided… for good claims experience."** Premium can go **down** for a clean record, but **never up** because you claimed |
| **Underwriting risk loadings (max %)** | Max % **not published** (unlike HDFC's 100%/150% caps). No medicals up to age 45 (if no adverse condition); loadings possible at entry for declared conditions — *unverified ceiling*. **Nil expected for a healthy 26-yo** |
| **Deductible-linked discount** | Voluntary **Aggregate Deductible** (₹50k/₹1L/₹2L/₹3L) → steep premium cut (grid shows up to ~44–72% off depending on SI/deductible). Trade-off: you self-pay small claims |
| **Voluntary co-pay discount** | Accept 10% / 20% co-pay → **10% / 20%** premium off |
| **Discount stack** | **Online/direct: 5%** (applies to this profile) · Family 10%/15% (N/A single) · Long-term 4% (2yr)/8% (3yr) prepay · Employee 20% (Bajaj staff only) · **Room-rent-capping: 8%** (accept 1.5%/₹7,500-day cap) · **Overall cap: max 55% total discount** |
| **Instalment-premium option** | Monthly/Quarterly/Half-yearly. Loading factors: monthly ×0.086 (≈+3.2%/yr), quarterly ×0.257 (≈+2.8%), semi-annual ×0.509 (≈+1.8%). ⚠️ On a claim, **all remaining instalments become due & are deducted from payout** (M6 trap) |
| **Wellness earn-back (net premium)** | Meet health metrics → **5% / 7.5% / 10%** (+2.5% for 10k steps) off *next* renewal. A discount, not cash; caps at 10–12.5% |
| **Section 80D effective cost** | Premium deductible up to **₹25,000/yr** (self, <60) under the **old tax regime** only. At a 30% slab, a ₹21,110 premium's *effective* post-tax cost ≈ **₹14,777** (saves ₹6,333). ⚠️ **New regime: no 80D benefit** — check your regime |
| **Zone-based pricing / zone co-pay** | Metro = **Zone A** (highest rate, but **no co-pay anywhere**). Buying cheaper Zone B/C (20%/30% off) triggers 15–20% co-pay if treated in a Zone-A metro (M2). **This profile: pay Zone A, no co-pay trap** |
| **GST on premium** | ✅ **0%** — individual health exempted from GST since **22 Sep 2025** (56th GST Council). Base premium = payable premium. ~15.3% cheaper than the old ×1.18 era |
| **Age-banded vs annual increase** | ✅ **Age-banded (5-yr bands)** — gentler & predictable *within* a band (better than annual step-ups) — **but** see the 8% escalation overlay below |
| **Medical-inflation / block-repricing risk** | 🚩 **Pre-announced 8%/yr base-rate escalation** (see red flag). Plus discretionary IRDAI-approved revisions with **3-month notice** |
| **Base-vs-(base+super top-up) cost** | Not modelled here — at ₹10L+ the single-base Platinum already gives no-room-cap + Super Bonus; a layered top-up is more relevant at ₹50L+ (revisit if premium escalation bites) |
| **Premium revision history** | Rising: the current chart already embeds an **8%/yr forward escalation** for risk-inception dates from **25 Oct 2026**; older UINs repriced across versions (V032021 → V072425 → V082526) |

## 🚩 RED FLAG — pre-announced 8%/year base-rate escalation

The rate chart carries this note verbatim:
> *"An 8% increase on the base rates will apply every year to all policies with RID* [Risk Inception Date] *starting October 25, 2026 onwards."*

**Why this matters (plain English).** Beyond the age-band jumps in the table above, Bajaj has **pre-announced that the base rates themselves rise 8% every year.** These *compound* and *stack on top of* age-banding. 8%/yr means the rate **doubles roughly every 9 years** from repricing *alone* — before you even change age band.

```
Effect of 8%/yr base escalation on the RATE itself (age-band jumps come ON TOP):
 Year 0  ██████ 1.00×
 Year 5  █████████ 1.47×
 Year 10 █████████████ 2.16×
 Year 20 ███████████████████████████ 4.66×
```

> This is the framework's "medical-inflation / block-repricing risk" — but **quantified and contractual**, not just a vague possibility. It's the single biggest long-term cost driver and it makes the "flat first decade" of the age-curve misleading: a 26-yo's real renewal cost climbs even while their age band doesn't. **Weigh this heavily** — a low entry premium can be eroded fast.

> The "today's chart" column is a firm floor from the binding rate table. Net message: **budget for roughly 1.5× the frozen-rate estimate over a decade.**

## SI-scaling summary (cost lens)
- **Per-lakh premium falls steeply with SI** → high SI is the value play (Finding 2).
- **Age-banding, discounts, GST-free, zone rules are identical across SI tiers** — only the rupee amount scales.
- The **8%/yr repricing** hits every tier equally in % terms, so it doesn't change *which* SI is best value — ₹25L–₹50L remains the sweet spot for a 26-yo.

## Module 4 score: **3.5 / 5**

**Rationale:** Genuinely attractive *entry* economics for a 26-yo — **near-flat age-loading for the first decade, steep per-lakh economies of scale (high SI is cheap), 0% GST, a rich discount stack (online 5% / wellness up to 12.5% / long-term / deductible), and an asymmetric claims-experience discount** (premium can fall for good history, never rise for claiming). Held back to 3.5 by the **pre-announced 8%/yr base-rate escalation** — a quantified, compounding block-repricing risk that doubles the *rate* every ~9 years on top of age-banding, plus **no published loading ceiling** and the **instalment-on-claim deduction trap**. Cheap to start, but the long-run cost trajectory is the real watch-item.
