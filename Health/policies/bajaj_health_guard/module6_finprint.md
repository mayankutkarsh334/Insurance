# Module 6 — Fine Print & Longevity

_Source: **binding wording** (UIN BAJHLIP25035V072425, `resources/wording_current.pdf`), Section E General Conditions + IRDAI circulars_
_Profile: Individual, age 26, metro tier-1 · SI ₹10L–₹1Cr_

> **Plain-English intro.** This module is the "will this still treat me fairly in 30 years, and are there hidden traps in the contract?" check. It's only 5% of the score — **but it carries an override veto:** a single deal-breaker here (no lifelong renewal, claim-based loading, or a product being chronically withdrawn) can sink an otherwise great plan. So the job is less "score every clause" and more "**is there a landmine?**"

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **Moratorium** | After 5 continuous years, the insurer can **no longer dispute your policy** (except for proven fraud) — even if you made an honest mistake on the form. A strong protection |
| **Lifelong renewability** | They must let you renew **every year for life** — they can't drop you because you got old or made claims |
| **Grace period** | A short window after your renewal due-date to still pay without losing your accumulated benefits |
| **Free-look** | A 30-day "try it and return it" window on a new policy for a full-ish refund |
| **Portability** | Your right to switch to another insurer while carrying over your waiting-period credit |

## Fine print & longevity checklist
| Item | Detail | Flag |
|------|--------|------|

## SI-scaling read
- Fine-print terms are **identical across all SI tiers** — renewability, moratorium, grace, free-look, cancellation don't change with SI.
- The only SI-linked mechanics: **(1) enhancement resets the moratorium + waiting** on the increased portion (so buy adequate SI *upfront* — reinforces M2/M4), and **(2)** the instalment-claim trap scales with the rupee premium (bigger at high SI).

## 🚩 Red flags & conflicts
- **No brochure-vs-wording conflict** in this module.
- Real (but non-veto) traps: **instalment-on-claim deduction**, **grace-period coverage gap**, **enhancement resets moratorium/waiting**, **harsh void+forfeit non-disclosure wording**, and the **withdrawal-and-migrate repricing risk** (the longevity counterpart to M4's pre-announced 8%/yr escalation). None disqualifying; most are avoidable (pay annually, buy high SI upfront, fill the form honestly).

## 🌱 NEW dimension added (Rule 3)
- **`study_plan.md` M6 — added bullet: "Grace-period coverage gap."** The framework listed "grace period" but not the crucial nuance that **many plans preserve your *continuity/credits* during grace yet do NOT pay claims arising *within* the grace window.** Bajaj: **no coverage during grace for annual payers** (only instalment-mode gets grace-window cover). Broad enough for all plans → added to the shared plan and applied here (row above).
- **Applied existing dimensions:** instalment-claim trap (HDFC M6), wellness-wallet forfeiture / lock-in (ABHI M6 — Bajaj *passes*, no wallet), and withdrawal-and-migrate re-pricing (ABHI MAX M6).
