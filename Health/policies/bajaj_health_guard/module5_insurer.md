# Module 5 — Insurer Quality

_Source: IRDAI + ICRA rating rationale + company disclosures. About the **company** (Bajaj General Insurance, formerly Bajaj Allianz General Insurance), not the specific plan._

> **Read this first — why the company matters.** You'll hold this policy for **decades** and may make your biggest claim 20–30 years out. The question isn't only "is the plan good?" but "**will this company still exist, stay solvent, and pay fairly** that far ahead?" This module grades financial strength, ownership backing, profitability, and track record.

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **Solvency ratio** | Safety cushion — how many times over the insurer can cover expected claims. Law requires **≥1.5×**; higher = safer |
| **Combined ratio** | Costs (claims + expenses) ÷ premiums. **Below 100% = the insurer makes an underwriting profit** before investment income; above 100% = it loses money on the policies themselves |
| **RoE** | Return on equity — how profitable it is for its owners. Higher & stable = healthier |
| **Credit rating (AAA)** | Independent grade of financial strength. **AAA = highest, safest tier** |
| **SAHI vs general insurer** | SAHI = health-only company. General insurer = health *plus* motor, travel, home, etc. |

## 🚩 Key nuance — the Allianz exit (ownership transition)

**What happened (plain English).** Until 2025 the company was a joint venture: **Bajaj Finserv + Allianz SE** (one of the world's largest insurers). In 2025 **Allianz sold its entire 26% stake** to the Bajaj Group for ~₹13,780 cr, and the company was **renamed from "Bajaj Allianz General Insurance" to "Bajaj General Insurance."** Bajaj Group now owns ~100%. Allianz is instead forming a *new* India venture with Jio Financial.

**Why it matters for a decades-long policy:**
- ➖ **Loss of a global insurance parent** — Allianz brought worldwide technical expertise, reinsurance depth and a foreign capital backstop. HDFC ERGO (the benchmark) *retains* Munich Re; Bajaj is now a **domestically-owned** insurer.
- ➕ **But Bajaj Finserv is a top-tier Indian financial group**, and — decisively — **ICRA reaffirmed the AAA rating *after* the exit**, with solvency at 3.25× and 16% RoE. The company clearly doesn't *need* Allianz's capital to stand alone.
- ➕ Full Bajaj control simplifies governance and ends JV-partner uncertainty.
- ⚠️ **Watch-item, not a red flag:** near-term there's brand/systems/rebranding transition, and long-term the tail-risk backstop is now domestic (GIC Re + global reinsurance panel) rather than a global insurance parent.

> **Net read:** financially Bajaj is *elite* — arguably the strongest balance sheet of the finalists (highest solvency, best combined ratio, AAA reaffirmed, 16% RoE). The **only** thing keeping it a notch below HDFC on this module is the **loss of the global insurance parent**, a real change to the "who stands behind it" thesis — mitigated by a rock-solid domestic group and a post-exit AAA reaffirmation.

## SI-scaling read
- Insurer strength is **identical whatever SI tier you buy** — a ₹10L and a ₹1Cr policyholder sit behind the same AAA balance sheet, the same 3.25× solvency, the same reinsurance panel.
- Only indirect link: **very large (high-SI) claims** lean harder on solvency/reinsurance — where the 3.25× solvency (highest of the finalists) and "lower retention on larger risks" (ICRA) are most reassuring. High-SI buyers are, if anything, *better* served by Bajaj's capital strength.
