## Verdict
**4.05 / 5 — a top-tier, claims-first choice; strongest on "will they pay," mid-pack on features & long-run cost.**

Bajaj Health Guard Platinum's core strength is **claims reliability** (M3 = 5/5): the **highest ICR of all finalists (87.3%, rising)**, the **lowest complaint ratio (3.42/10k)**, in-house/no-TPA claims, and an **elite balance sheet** (M5 — highest solvency 3.25×, AAA reaffirmed post-Allianz-exit). For a 26-year-old who wants the surest payer for a decades-long hold, it's a benchmark.

Where it trails the HDFC frame: **coverage has real gaps** (M1 3.5 — no consumables/non-medical buy-back, 90-day post-hosp vs 180, no domiciliary/day-1-chronic, Recharge ₹5L-capped at high SI) and **long-run cost carries a pre-announced 8%/yr base-rate escalation** (M4 3.5) that compounds on top of age-banding. Exclusions (M2 4) and fine print (M6 4) are clean and buyer-friendly with **no override veto**.

**Best fit:** buy **Platinum at a high SI (₹25L–₹50L sweet spot) young**, pay **annually** (avoid the instalment trap), in **Zone A** (no co-pay). **Watch-items:** the 8%/yr repricing trajectory and the post-Allianz ownership transition — neither disqualifying.
