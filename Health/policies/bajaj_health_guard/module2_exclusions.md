# Module 2 — Exclusions & Waiting Periods

_Source: Health Guard **current binding wording** (UIN BAJHLIP25035V072425, 2024-25), Sections A/C/D/E_
_Profile: Individual, age 26, metro tier-1 (Zone A) · SI tiers ₹10L / ₹25L / ₹50L / ₹1Cr_

> **Plain-English intro.** Module 1 was "what you get." Module 2 is **"what they can refuse to pay, and when."** Three things decide most denials: (1) **waiting periods** — how long before a given illness is covered; (2) **sub-limits / co-pay** — caps and cost-sharing that shrink a payout even for a covered claim; (3) **exclusions** — things never paid. A healthy 26-year-old mostly cares about the *waiting periods* (you'll likely claim years from now) and the *non-disclosure* rules (an honest proposal form protects you).

## ⏳ Waiting periods — the three-tier ladder (visual)

Health Guard has **three separate waiting tiers**, not two. This is easy to misread:

```
 Day 0 ──30 days──────── 24 months ─────────── 36 months ───────────────────► covered
   │        │                  │                     │
   │   ACCIDENTS covered from day 1 (all tiers)      │
   │        │                  │                     │
   ├─ 30-day initial wait (Excl03): any illness in first 30 days not paid
   │        (waived if you had >12 mo continuous cover)
   │                           │                     │
   ├──────── 24-month "specified disease" list (Excl02): 27 conditions
   │         cataract, hernia, piles, hydrocele, gallbladder/cholecystitis,
   │         CKD, Alzheimer's, mental illness, sinuses, fibroids, etc.
   │                                                 │
   ├──────── 36-month PED wait (Excl01): any pre-existing disease
   │                                                 │
   └──────── 36-month "first 3 years" list: joint replacement, spinal/vertebral
             surgery, deviated nasal septum, congenital-internal, refractive ≥7.5D,
             bariatric, Parkinson's, genetic disorders
```

| Item | Detail | Concern |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Initial (30-day) waiting** | Any illness in first 30 days excluded; **accidents exempt**; waived if >12 mo continuous cover | Standard, mild |
| **PED waiting** | **36 months** | ⚠️ **No buy-down offered** — Health Guard has *no* paid PED-reduction rider (unlike some plans). Fixed 36 mo; only portability credit shortens it. Not reducible by customer *or* channel — the reduction simply doesn't exist. 36 mo is already market-standard, so mild concern |
| **Specific-disease waiting (list)** | **24 months**, **27 conditions** (extracted below) | Common day-to-day surgeries (cataract, hernia, piles, gallstones) wait 2 yrs |
| **Third tier — "first 3 years" list** | **36 months** for joint replacement, spinal surgery, bariatric, Parkinson's, genetic, congenital-internal, refractive ≥7.5D, deviated septum/turbinate | ⚠️ **Joint replacement & spine wait 36 mo, not 24** — longer than the "specific-disease" list. Matters more at older age; low relevance at 26 |
| **Maternity waiting** | **72 months** (6 years), Gold/Platinum only | Very long; down-weighted for this profile |
| **Enhancement-of-SI resets waiting** | ✅ Yes — PED/specific/30-day **and the 60-mo moratorium** all reset *on the increased slab* | ⚠️ **Compounding trap:** raising SI later restarts the clock on the top-up portion → buy adequate SI **upfront** |

**The 27 specified-disease conditions (24-mo wait):** gastrointestinal ulcers · cataracts · fistula · macular degeneration · benign prostatic hypertrophy · hernia (all) · sinuses · fissure-in-ano · haemorrhoids/piles · hydrocele · dysfunctional uterine bleeding · fibromyoma · endometriosis · hysterectomy · uterine prolapse · urinary/biliary stones · ENT/tonsil/adenoid/sinus surgery · tumours/cysts/nodules/polyps (incl. breast lumps, benign) · **mental illness** · gallbladder/cholecystitis · pancreatitis · cirrhosis (all) · gout & rheumatism · tonsillitis · varicose veins · **chronic kidney disease** · **Alzheimer's**.

## Co-pay, sub-limits & cost-sharing

| Item | Detail | Concern |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Co-pay (mandatory/age-based)** | ❌ **None** — no forced co-pay at any entry age | ✅ Buyer-friendly (many plans force 10-20% co-pay for older entry) |
| **Co-pay (voluntary)** | Optional 10%/20% opt-in → premium discount (feeds M4) | Only if *you* choose it for a cheaper premium |
| **Zone-based co-pay** | **Zone A (metro) buyer = NO co-pay anywhere.** Buy cheaper Zone B/C then treat in Zone A → **15%/20% co-pay** | ⚠️ Trap only if you *under-buy* your zone. **This profile is metro → buy Zone A → no trap.** Accident cases exempt |
| **Deductible (aggregate)** | Voluntary ₹50k/₹1L/₹2L/₹3L options → premium discount | Optional, not default |
| **Cataract** | 20% of SI/eye, **max ₹1L**, after 24-mo wait | Rupee cap bites at SI > ₹5L |
| **Bariatric** | 50% of SI, **max ₹5L**, 36-mo wait | Rupee cap bites at SI > ₹10L |
| **Modern treatments (12 mandated)** | ✅ **Full Base Sum Insured — NO sub-limit** | ✅ **Corrected from M1** — 2021 wording capped at 50%/₹5L; **current binding wording removed the cap** (robotic/oral-chemo/immunotherapy/deep-brain-stim etc. up to full SI). Strong positive |
| **Mental-illness coverage (parity?)** | ✅ **Full Base Sum Insured** (24-mo specific-disease wait applies) | ✅ **Corrected from M1** — 2021 capped at 25%/₹2L; **current wording gives full-SI parity** (Annexure IV ICD list). Not neutered by sub-limit |
| **Maternity** | ₹25k normal / ₹35k caesarean (SI ≥ ₹10L), 72-mo wait | Low limits; down-weighted |

> ✅ **Wording-version win:** the two big IRDAI-sensitive sub-limits (mental illness, modern treatments) that the *2021* wording capped are **full-SI in the current wording**. This is exactly why *wording binds, not the brochure or old copies* — and why the version on file matters.

## Permanent exclusions (the "never paid" catalogue — ~25 items)

Standard IRDAI-aligned list. Notable ones: cosmetic/plastic surgery (unless accident/burn/cancer reconstruction) · obesity surgery (unless strict BMI criteria) · change-of-gender · sterility & infertility/IVF · dental (unless accidental + hospitalised) · refractive error < 7.5 dioptres · spectacles/contact lenses/hearing aids · **external equipment incl. CPAP / home dialysis (C.P.A.D) / oxygen concentrator** · dietary supplements (unless prescribed in-claim) · unproven/experimental treatment · alcoholism/substance abuse · health-hydros/spas · congenital *external* defects · intentional self-injury · **all non-medical items (Annexure II)** · **any treatment outside India** · circumcision (unless medically needed) · treatment other than allopathy/AYUSH · admission purely for investigation/evaluation · rest-cure/custodial care.

| Item | Detail | Concern |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Underwriting-imposed permanent exclusion** | Insurer *may* accept a proposal but attach a **specific permanent exclusion** on a declared PED (standard IRDAI counter-offer). No detailed counter-offer clause spelled out, but Excl01(d) requires PED "declared and accepted" | ⚠️ Low risk for a **healthy 26-yo with a clean proposal**; matters only if you declare a condition |
| **Individual claim-based loading** | ❌ **Banned** — "loadings on renewal premium shall be at portfolio, not based on any individual policy" | ✅ You won't be singled out for a premium hike after claiming (feeds M6) |
| **Non-disclosure consequences** | Policy **void + all premium forfeited** on misrepresentation/non-disclosure of material fact; **fraud** → all benefits + premium forfeited, paid claims recoverable | ⚠️ Fill the proposal form **honestly & completely.** Protected by 60-mo **moratorium**: after 60 months no contest *except established fraud*; also can't repudiate if you prove no deliberate intent (feeds M6) |

## 📊 How exclusions/waiting scale with SI (₹10L → ₹1Cr)

| Dimension | ₹10L | ₹25L | ₹50L | ₹1Cr | Scales? |
|-----------|:----:|:----:|:----:|:----:|:-------:|
| PED / specific / 30-day waits | 36/24/1 mo | same | same | same | ❌ SI-independent |
| Mandatory co-pay | None | None | None | None | ✅ none at any SI |
| Zone-A co-pay | None | None | None | None | ✅ (metro buyer) |
| Modern treatment cap | Full SI | Full SI | Full SI | Full SI | ✅ scales cleanly |
| Mental illness cap | Full SI | Full SI | Full SI | Full SI | ✅ scales cleanly |
| Cataract (20%/eye, ₹1L cap) | ₹1L (10%) | ₹1L (4%) | ₹1L (2%) | ₹1L (1%) | ⚠️ flat ₹1L cap shrinks |
| Bariatric (50%, ₹5L cap) | ₹5L | ₹5L | ₹5L (10%) | ₹5L (5%) | ⚠️ flat ₹5L cap shrinks |

> **Takeaway:** waiting periods and co-pay are **identical across all SI tiers** — buying more cover doesn't buy *shorter* waits or *less* co-pay. The only SI-sensitive items are the **flat-rupee sub-limits** (cataract ₹1L, bariatric ₹5L), which — like M1's Recharge/modern-treatment caps — become trivially small at ₹50L/₹1Cr. Since these are minor procedures, the impact is low.

## 🚩 Red flags & brochure-vs-wording conflicts
1. **No PED waiting buy-down** — 36 mo is fixed; you cannot pay to shorten it (some competitors let you reduce PED to 24/12 mo). Only portability credit helps.
2. **Enhancement resets the clock** — raising SI later restarts PED/specific/30-day waits **and the 60-mo moratorium** on the increased portion. Buy adequate SI upfront.
3. **Three-tier waiting** — joint replacement / spine / bariatric sit in a **36-month** list, not the 24-month one (low relevance at 26).
4. **Wording-version drift (positive):** the *current* wording removed the mental-illness (25%/₹2L) and modern-treatment (50%/₹5L) caps that the 2021 wording carried → both now **full-SI**. Always check the *current* wording UIN.
5. **No mandatory/age co-pay, no individual claim-loading, Zone-A = no co-pay** — all buyer-friendly for this profile.
