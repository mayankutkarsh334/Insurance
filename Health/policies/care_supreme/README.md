## Verdict

**Final weighted score: 3.50 / 5. No override veto. A genuinely good *product* sold by the study's weakest *insurer*.**

Care Supreme's **contract is strong and unusually clean**. It is a **single plan** — no variant ladder, so none of the mis-selling hazards that bite SBI and Bajaj — and it delivers **uncapped room and ICU at every SI tier**, **60/180 pre/post-hospitalisation** (matching the HDFC benchmark and beating SBI's 90-day post), a **claim-proof Cumulative Bonus**, **Unlimited Automatic Recharge for related *and* unrelated illness** with no relapse trap, and **no sub-limits on modern treatments, AYUSH, day-care or organ donor**. On exclusions it has **no mandatory, age- or zone-triggered co-pay**, **no hidden third waiting tier**, and — the standout — **no pre-policy medical tests up to age 65 at ANY sum insured**, so a healthy 26-year-old can buy ₹50L–₹1Cr with zero underwriting, zero loading exposure and no permanent-carve-out surface. On cost it is **competitively priced, GST-free, age-banded in 5-year steps, carries no contractual escalator** (against Bajaj's printed +8%/yr) and **cannot load your premium for claiming**.

**The problem is the company, not the policy.** Module 5 found **solvency declining three years running to 1.68×** — only 0.18 above IRDAI's floor and the thinnest of any finalist — alongside **FY25 claims growing 33% against 21% premium growth, operating profit down 86% and PAT halved**, an **IRDAI ₹1 crore penalty** for granting ESOPs after the regulator had expressly refused them, and the **weakest credit rating of the finalists (`IND AA-` vs AAA for SBI General and Bajaj)** after CRISIL's rating was withdrawn at Care's own request. Because Care is a **monoline SAHI it cannot cross-subsidise a bad health year**: its only two levers are **repricing** and **paying less** — and **both are already visible**. Module 3 measured the second directly: a **96.74% claim settlement ratio by count sitting beside a 64.53% ICR by rupees**, with **42 complaints per 10,000 claims — the worst in the study, ~8.4× HDFC's 4.99**. Module 4 showed the first is **unauditable**, because Care is the only finalist that **publishes no rate chart**. Module 2 catalogued the machinery that makes bill-trimming possible: R&C and Medically-Necessary discretion, a broad **"illness *attributable to* tobacco/alcohol/smoking"** permanent exclusion that even the 60-month moratorium does not neutralise, RMO/service/night charges, and **consumables sold only as a paid rider**.

**The honest read: Care will very probably pay your claim — it is the finalist you are most likely to have to argue with about *how much*.** Its low price is partly financed by exactly that friction.

**If chosen, buy it deliberately:**
1. **Add the Claim Shield rider** — it closes the consumables/non-payables deduction channel, the single biggest source of "why did they cut my bill?" disputes.
2. **Buy the large SI at entry (₹25L–₹50L)** — no medical tests to 65 at any SI, and enhancing later restarts every waiting clock *and* the moratorium on the increase.
3. **Refuse the risk-transfer "discounts"** — Smart Select (20% co-pay off-list), True Connect (10% gatekeeper co-pay), voluntary co-pay, Room Rent Modification. Verify the schedule doesn't carry them silently.
4. **Pay annually and renew before the due date** — coverage does *not* run during the 30-day grace period, and instalments trigger the claim-acceleration trap.
5. **Notify every address and job change in writing** (§5.18 condition precedent — sharpened by 7-zone pricing), and **intimate any admission within 48 hours** (§6.1.4 condition precedent).
6. ✅ **Keep the exit open** — Care's wellness reward is a *discount*, not a forfeitable wallet, so porting costs you nothing. Given the repricing pressure in M5, that option has real value.

**Comparative placement:** with the Stage-2 corrections raised by this study (**Network 4 → 3** from M3, **Stability 4 → 2.5** from M5), Care's screening total falls **24/30 → ~21.5/30**, putting it **last among the five finalists**. Its 3.50 weighted score reflects a plan whose **coverage would rank near the top and whose insurer ranks at the bottom** — the reverse of the profile a 40-year buyer wants, since the contract can be ported away but the claims culture is what you live with.
