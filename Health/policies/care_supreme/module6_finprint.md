# Module 6 — Fine Print & Longevity

_Source: **Care Supreme policy wording** (UIN **CHIHLIP27061V032627**, v03) — **Section 5 General Terms & Clauses (5.1–5.26)**, §6.1 claims procedure, Annexure IV (Ombudsman offices); IRDAI Master Circular 29-May-2024. Files in `resources/`._
_Profile studied: **Individual (single adult), age 26, metro tier-1** — a **40–60 year** relationship._
_**Weight: 5% — but this module holds the OVERRIDE VETO.** A deal-breaker here disqualifies the plan regardless of its total._

> **Plain-English intro.** You are 26. This policy may need to still be paying claims when you are 70. This module asks: **"Can they ever throw me out? Can they quietly change the deal? And what small print could cost me a claim decades from now?"**
> - **Renewability** = can they refuse to renew you? (It must be *lifelong*.)
> - **Moratorium** = after 5 continuous years, they can no longer dig into your past to contest a claim.
> - **Grace period** = the days after your due date in which you can still pay. ⚠️ **Being "in grace" is not the same as being covered** — see the finding below.
> - **Condition precedent** = a duty you must perform, or they can refuse the claim outright.

## 🚩 The grace-period gap, drawn

> **What most people assume:** "I have 30 days to pay after the due date, so I'm covered for those 30 days." **That is wrong here.**

```
   Policy due date
        │
        ▼
  ══════╪═══════════ 30-day GRACE PERIOD ═══════════╪══════════
        │  ❌ NOT COVERED (annual payers)           │  ✅ covered again
        │  ✅ Continuity/Cumulative Bonus PRESERVED │     once renewed
        │                                            │
        │  ⚠️ A hospitalisation HERE is not payable  │
        │                                            │
        └─ but INSTALMENT payers ARE covered here ───┘  (§5.13.2)
```
> **Finding.** §5.10(iii) preserves your **credits** through the grace window but **not your cover**. **Practical rule: renew ON or BEFORE the due date — treat the grace period as a credit-protection device, not a cover extension.** ✅ Care's **early-renewal discount (5% if you pay 31+ days early, 2.5% within 30 days — M4)** conveniently pays you to avoid this trap.

## 🆕 NEW DIMENSION discovered in this module *(Rule 3)*

### Ongoing "material change" disclosure as a condition precedent — the relocation/occupation trap *(→ new bullet in study_plan M6; new row above)*
The framework tests disclosure thoroughly **at inception** — the non-disclosure remedies bullet, the underwriting permanent-exclusion check (HDFC M2), and M3's new intimation-condition-precedent check. **But it never asks whether the policyholder carries a CONTINUING duty of disclosure for the next forty years.** Care Supreme §5.18 imposes exactly that, and makes it a **condition precedent to liability**: you must **immediately notify in writing** any material change in **occupation, business, or current residing address**, and Care may then **adjust cover, adjust premium, or reject the claim**.

For this buyer the clause is unusually live: **a 26-year-old will change employer and city repeatedly before 66**, and because **Care prices across 7 zones (M4), an address change is directly a pricing event** — so the notification duty is not administrative housekeeping but a premium-affecting obligation the insurer has a financial interest in enforcing. Reinforcing it, **§5.20 "No constructive Notice"** provides that anything Care happens to know but which was not **expressly disclosed in writing** does not bind it — so informally telling an agent is worth nothing.
> **New generalisable check: "Does the wording impose a CONTINUING duty to notify material changes (occupation, residence, health) during the policy term — and is it drafted as a condition precedent to liability? Weight it higher for zone-priced products, where a change of address is itself a pricing event, and for long holds where relocation is near-certain."** **Buyer rule: notify every address and occupation change in writing, and keep the acknowledgement.**

## Brochure-vs-wording check *(Rule 2)*

✅ **No conflict.** The wording, prospectus and current brochure agree on renewability (lifelong), grace period (30 days), free look (30 days), moratorium (5 years), portability/migration windows and the grievance ladder.

⚠️ **Two things the marketing does not surface** (not contradictions — omissions the buyer must read the wording to find):
1. **"30-day grace period"** is promoted as a convenience; **only the wording discloses that coverage does not run during it** for annual payers.
2. **The instalment option** is marketed as affordability; **only §5.13(6)–(7) discloses that a claim accelerates all remaining instalments and lets Care net them off your payout**, and that the tenure discount is forfeited.

> **Carry-forward flags** *(stage2_shortlist.md)*: Care's M3 flag was **resolved** in Module 3 (ICR defused via peer-segment benchmarking; complaints confirmed at 42/10k). **M6 adds no new flag but sharpens one existing risk:** M5's repricing pressure meets M6's §5.14 revision power and §5.11 withdraw-and-migrate route — **continuity is contractually protected, price is not.** ✅ **The mitigant is that Care's wellness reward is a discount rather than a forfeitable wallet, so the buyer keeps a clean, cost-free option to PORT if repricing arrives** — which, given M5, is a genuinely valuable escape hatch.

**Override veto triggered?** ⬜ **no** — lifelong renewability confirmed, claim-based loading expressly banned, no chronic withdrawal.

**Module 6 score (1–5): 3.5 / 5**

**Rationale.** The **structural longevity protections are all in place and none of the three veto triggers fires**: **lifelong renewability** (entry *and* exit age unlimited), an express ban on **claim-based renewal loading** and on refusing renewal because you claimed, a standard **60-month moratorium**, a **30-day free look**, **pro-rata cancellation refunds**, IRDAI-compliant **migration and portability with full continuity credit**, **90 days' notice plus a one-time migrate-with-continuity right** if the product is withdrawn, a **policyholder-favourable multiple-policy clause** (valuable if you also hold corporate cover), and a **fully specified grievance ladder** to Ombudsman and Bima Bharosa. Care also **passes the SBI M6 wellness-structure test outright**: its up-to-30% reward is a **renewal discount, not an accrued wallet**, so there is **nothing to forfeit on exit** — and given Module 5's repricing pressure, keeping a **cost-free option to port** is worth more here than in any other finalist.

It is held to 3.5 by four sharp edges, none disqualifying but all worth pricing in. **(1) The grace-period coverage gap** — §5.10(iii) preserves your credits but **not your cover** during the 30 days, and perversely **instalment payers are covered while annual payers are not**. **(2) The instalment claim trap** — a claim makes every remaining instalment fall due and Care may net them off your payout, while the tenure discount is forfeited outright. **(3) The material-change condition precedent (§5.18)** — a continuing, forty-year duty to notify occupation and **address** changes in writing, made materially riskier by Care's **7-zone pricing** (an address change *is* a pricing event) and hardened by §5.20's "no constructive notice"; it joins claim intimation (M3) and §5.2 to give Care an **unusual stack of independently claim-defeating condition precedents**. **(4) Renewability is not a price guarantee** — §5.14's revision power is where M5's collapsing margins and M4's absent rate chart converge. Non-disclosure remains a binary **void-and-forfeit-all-premium** rather than the softer three-option remedy ladder, and the **moratorium expressly does not neutralise permanent exclusions**, so M2's broad tobacco/alcohol-attributable carve-out survives all five years and beyond.
