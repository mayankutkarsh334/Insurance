# Module 6 — Fine Print & Longevity

_Source: **binding policy wording** `my:Optima Secure` (UIN HDFHLIP26058V082526, `resources/`), Section D General Conditions §1.6–1.16 + IRDAI norms. Wording governs._

> **Read this first — what is "fine print & longevity"?**
> This is the small-print in the contract that decides whether the insurer can **drop you, deny you late, or short-change a refund** years down the line. For a policy you'll keep for decades, these clauses matter as much as the benefits — they're the difference between "covered for life" and "covered until it's inconvenient for them."

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **Lifelong renewability** | They must let you renew every year for life — they can't drop you for getting old or sick |
| **Moratorium** | After 5 years, the insurer can **no longer question your old disclosures** — your cover becomes "unquestionable" (except proven fraud) |
| **Grace period** | Extra days to pay a late renewal without losing your policy or your waiting-period credit |
| **Free-look** | A 30-day "try it and return it" window right after you buy |
| **Portability / migration** | Your right to switch insurer/plan **without losing** the waiting periods you've already served |
| **Claim-based loading** | Hiking *your* premium just because you claimed — **banned** by IRDAI |

## SI-scaling read
- All fine-print rights are **identical across SI tiers** — a ₹10L and a ₹1Cr policyholder get the same renewal, moratorium, grace and portability protections.
- The one SI-linked catch: **enhancing your SI later restarts the 5-year moratorium (and the waiting periods, per M2) on the added cover** — another reason a 26-yo is better off buying a larger base up front than topping up piecemeal.
