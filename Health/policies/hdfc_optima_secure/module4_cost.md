# Module 4 — Cost & Premium

_Source: HDFC ERGO brochure/quote anchors + binding wording (§1.21 loadings, §1.24 Premium Tier). Exact age×SI tables live in the quote engine — figures marked **(indicative)** must be confirmed there; only sourced anchors are exact._

> **Read this first — what am I actually paying for?**
> The **premium** is the yearly rent you pay the insurer to stay covered. It is **not fixed for life** — it goes up as you age, and the insurer can revise the whole product's rates. You pay it every year, forever. This module asks: *how much, how fast does it rise, and how do I pay less without losing cover?*

## Plain-English glossary (terms used below)
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **Premium** | The yearly price of the policy |
| **Sum Insured (SI)** | The maximum the insurer will pay in a year (your cover size) |
| **Premium Tier / Zone** | Your city's price band. Big metros (Delhi) = most expensive; small towns = cheapest |
| **Deductible** | An amount *you* agree to pay first each year; in exchange the premium drops. Like a "voluntary excess" |
| **Loading** | A **surcharge** the insurer adds if you have a health risk (e.g. high BP) — makes *your* premium higher than the chart |
| **No-Claim / Favourable-Claims Discount** | A price cut you earn for **not** claiming; you lose it if you claim |
| **GST** | An 18% government tax added on top of the premium |
| **Section 80D** | A tax rule: the premium you pay is subtracted from your taxable income, so you save some tax back |

## Premium dynamics (the levers that change your bill)
| Lever | Detail (plain-English) |
|-------|------------------------|
| Claims-experience discount (premium ↑/↓ with claims) | **Favourable Claims Experience Discount**: up to **21%** off as a first-time buyer, up to **18%** at renewal *if you don't claim*. Claiming shrinks this discount → a legal, soft "you claimed, you pay a bit more" (outright claim-loading is banned) |
| Underwriting risk loadings (max %) | If you declare a health condition, insurer may surcharge up to **100% per condition / 150% per person** (§1.21). Healthy 26-yo → usually none |
| Deductible-linked discount | Agree to pay the first ₹25k–₹3L of claims yourself → premium drops **22.5% up to 65%**. Trade-off: more out-of-pocket if you do claim |
| Discount stack (family / loyalty / long-term / lifetime) | Family 10% · Loyalty 2.5% (existing HDFC customer) · Long-term 6% (2-yr) / 8% (3-yr paid upfront) · Lifetime 5% (first-time buyer, age ≤35 — **you qualify at 26**) |
| Instalment-premium option | Pay monthly/quarterly/half-yearly; **no-cost EMI** on debit/credit cards — spreads the cost, same total |
| Wellness earn-back (net premium) | **None** — HDFC gives discounts, but **no** activity-based premium-back (unlike Aditya Birla HealthReturns). A genuine cost gap vs that finalist |
| Section 80D effective cost | Premium (incl. GST) is tax-deductible up to **₹25,000/yr** (self, under 60). In the 30% tax slab that's up to **₹7,500 back** → real cost = premium − tax saved |
| Zone-based pricing / zone co-pay | City sets the price: **Delhi = Tier 1 (most expensive)**; a Tier-4 city pays materially less for the *same* plan. **No** zone co-pay (M2) |
| GST on premium (18%) | The quoted base premium is **before tax**; you actually pay **base × 1.18**. e.g. ₹20,000 base → **₹23,600** payable |
| Age-banded vs annual increase | **Age-banded** — premium steps up at band boundaries (e.g. 25/35/45…), not every single year |
| Medical-inflation / block-repricing risk | The real long-term threat: insurer re-prices the *whole* product every few years (medical inflation ~14%/yr). UIN `...V082526` = **version 08** → the product has been refiled several times. Exact past hike % *unverified* — ask HDFC for the revision history |
| Base-vs-(base+super top-up) cost | For a 26-yo wanting ₹1Cr cover, a **₹10L base + ₹90L super top-up** is usually far cheaper than a ₹1Cr base — see study_plan *Coverage Architecture*. Price both |
| Premium revision history | Multiple product versions (v01→v08); periodic rate revisions with 3-month notice (M6). Exact figures *unverified* |

## How your final bill is built (follow the arrows)
```
Base rate (for your AGE + SI)
        │  ×  city TIER (Delhi = highest)
        ▼
   + risk LOADING (only if you declare an illness)
        │
        ▼
   − DISCOUNTS (lifetime 5% + long-term + family + not-claiming …)
        │
        ▼
   + 18% GST
        ▼
   = AMOUNT YOU PAY  ──►  − Section 80D tax saved  =  REAL cost
```

## Projected lifetime outgo (indicative)
- **10-yr outgo (age 26→35):** the cheapest decade — roughly **10× a low-20s-thousand premium**, rising gently (see index). *(indicative — confirm with quote)*
- **20-yr outgo (26→45):** still moderate; the steep climb is *after* 50. Starting at 26 front-loads the cheap years — the single biggest cost lever is simply **buying young**.

## Module 4 score: **4 / 5**
Not the cheapest — it's a **Tier-1-metro, feature-rich plan**, so the sticker premium runs above bare-bones rivals — but the **cost *levers* are excellent**: up to 65% deductible discount, a 5% lifetime discount you qualify for at 26, no-cost EMI, and 80D tax relief. The two drags are **no wellness earn-back** (a real gap vs Aditya Birla) and **no published individual rate table** (scored on structure + anchors; revisit if the quote comes in high). Buying at 26 captures the cheap decades — the biggest lever of all.
