> **Read this first — what am I actually paying for?**
> The **premium** is the yearly rent you pay to stay covered. It is **not fixed for life** — it rises as you age, and the insurer can re-price the whole product. You pay it every year, forever. This module asks: *how much, how fast does it rise, and how do I pay less without losing cover?*

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **Premium** | The yearly price of the policy |
| **Sum Insured (SI)** | The maximum the insurer will pay in a year (your cover size) |
| **Premium Tier / Zone** | Your city's price band. Big metros (Delhi, Mumbai) = most expensive; small towns = cheapest |
| **Deductible** | An amount *you* agree to pay first each year; in exchange the premium drops. Like a "voluntary excess" |
| **Loading** | A **surcharge** added if you have a health risk (e.g. high BP) — makes *your* premium higher than the chart |
| **Escalator** | A **contractually printed** annual price rise. Bajaj prints +8%/yr. **Its absence is worth more than a low entry price** |
| **Age band** | Whether the price steps up every 5 years (gentler) or every single year (harsher) |
| **GST** | Government tax. ✅ **Now 0% on individual health insurance** — it used to be 18% |
| **Section 80D** | A tax rule: the premium is subtracted from your taxable income, so you save some tax back |

## 🚩 CORRECTION — GST is now **0%**, not 18%

The previous version of this module applied **18% GST** and stated *"₹20,000 base → ₹23,600 payable."* **That is no longer correct.**

> **The GST Council (56th meeting, 3 Sept 2025) exempted all individual life and health insurance from GST, effective 22 September 2025.** You now pay the **base premium only** — a **~15% real price drop** against the old base × 1.18. ⚠️ **Group/employer cover is still taxed at 18%.**

**Every figure below is GST-free and final.** This change is **industry-wide**, so it improves HDFC's absolute cost but **does not change its ranking** against the other finalists.

- **10-yr outgo (age 26 → 35):** the cheapest decade. On the ₹13,459 anchor with age-banding and no escalator, roughly **₹1.4–1.6 lakh over ten years before any block revision** — *assumption-driven estimate, not a quote.*

## Module 4 score: **4 / 5** *(unchanged after re-testing)*

**Rationale.** HDFC is **not the cheapest plan in the study and does not pretend to be** — at roughly **₹897 per lakh of cover it prices level with Care Supreme, and ACKO sells ₹1 crore for about what HDFC charges for ₹15 lakh**. Its cost case rests instead on **structure**, and there the levers are genuinely strong: **no contractually printed escalator** (against Bajaj's +8%/yr, which doubles base rates every ~9 years and dominates any entry-price gap over a 40-year hold); **age-banded rather than annual re-pricing**; the **deepest deductible discount in the study (22.5%–65%)**; a **5% lifetime discount you qualify for at 26**; **no-cost EMI**; **no zone co-pay** despite six premium tiers; and — newly corrected — **GST at 0% since September 2025**, making the quoted premium the final price and cutting the real cost ~15% against the old regime. It is also the **only plan that publishes a ceiling on underwriting loadings** (100%/condition, 150%/person) where Care, SBI and ACKO leave it open-ended.

Three things hold it at 4/5. **First, and newly weighted: HDFC is the only finalist with claim-linked pricing** — a **Favourable Claims Experience Discount of up to 21%/18% that you lose by claiming**. Outright claim loading is banned, but a discount forfeited on claiming is economically the same penalty, and **Care, SBI and ACKO all expressly have no such mechanism in either direction**. **Second, there is no wellness earn-back at all**, where Aditya Birla, Care and SBI each offer up to ~30% back. **Third, no public individual rate chart exists**, so the age curve cannot be verified before purchase and a future hike has no published baseline to be audited against — though HDFC is **better than ACKO here**, since its pricing is **tabular and age-banded rather than algorithmic**. Add a **v08 filing history** — the most-revised product in this study, with unverified past hike percentages — and the honest read is: **a fairly-priced, well-structured premium with excellent discount levers and no escalator, but the weakest value-per-lakh of the finalists and the only one that quietly charges you for claiming.**
