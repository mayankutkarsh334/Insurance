# Module 6 — Fine Print & Longevity

_Source: **Super Health policy wording** (UIN **SBIHLIP24141V022324**) §G General Terms & Clauses + §E/CIS + prospectus. Weight: **5% — but carries the OVERRIDE VETO** (a deal-breaker here disqualifies the plan regardless of the weighted score)._
_Profile studied: **Individual (single adult), age 26, metro tier-1** — a **40-year** hold. This is the module a 40-year horizon cares about most._

> **Plain-English intro.** The first five modules asked what the policy pays *today*. This one asks the questions that only matter **decades from now**: **Can they refuse to renew me when I'm old and sick? If I renew a day late, am I still covered? Can they cancel on a technicality? Can I switch away if they misbehave — without losing the waiting-period credit I've built up?** These clauses never come up until the one moment they decide everything. Two terms:
> - **Moratorium** = after **5 continuous years**, the insurer can **no longer question or reject your policy for non-disclosure** (except proven fraud). Your cover becomes "incontestable."
> - **Portability** = your legal right to **switch insurer while keeping all the years of waiting periods you've already served** — your escape hatch if this insurer later disappoints.

## 🕳️ The two real longevity traps (both survive the veto but matter over 40 years)

### 1. The grace-period coverage gap — *the Bajaj-M6 trap, confirmed*
```
   ANNUAL payer, renewal due 1-Jan, pays 20-Jan (within 30-day grace):
     Continuity / NCB / waiting credits ...... PRESERVED ✅
     A hospitalisation on 10-Jan .............. NOT COVERED 🚩
        "Coverage is not available during the Grace Period" (§G.j.v)

   INSTALMENT payer, same 10-Jan event in grace window:
     COVERED ✅ — §G.m.ii grants cover during the instalment grace window
```
> **Plain English:** if you pay **annually** and renew even one day late, you keep all your accrued benefits **but you are uninsured until you actually pay.** A claim in that window is denied. **This is the single most likely way a diligent policyholder gets burned** — a forgotten renewal date, not a fine-print exclusion. **Rule: renew ON TIME, ideally by auto-debit; never lean on the grace period as if it were cover.** (Odd but true: the *instalment* payer is better protected in the grace window than the annual payer.)

### 2. Renewability ≠ price guarantee — *the ABHI-MAX-M6 trap, weighted up*
"Lifelong renewability" guarantees SBI will **keep offering** you a policy — it does **not** guarantee the **price**. Two channels let the price move regardless:
- **§G.n block re-pricing:** SBI may, with IRDAI approval and 3 months' notice, revise premium rates for the **whole cohort** (M4 — revision history unverified).
- **§G.k withdrawal-and-migrate:** if the product is withdrawn, you migrate to a *"similar"* product with continuity credits but **at that product's rate.**

> **Why this bites harder for *this* plan (M5 linkage):** SBI General's **retail health is only ~12.6% of its book**, and the health line sits inside a company running a **109.6% combined ratio**. A small, loss-making line is a **more-than-average candidate for withdrawal or aggressive re-pricing.** The renewability guarantee is real and protects your *access* and *credits* — but **budget for the price to be re-set over a 40-year hold.** A caution for the decision tree, not a reason to reject.

## 🆕 NEW DIMENSION discovered in this module *(Rule 3)*

### Wellness benefit STRUCTURE — discount vs accrued wallet — determines exit lock-in *(→ new bullet in study_plan M6)*
The framework's ABHI-M6 dimension flags that a **wellness *wallet*** (HealthReturns-style accrued balance) **forfeits on porting/cancelling → locks you in**, and the more you value it the more trapped you are. This module found the **mirror-image, benign case worth scoring explicitly**: SBI's **Walk Healthy is a straight renewal-premium *discount* (up to 30%), with no accrued balance**, so **there is nothing to forfeit and no lock-in.** The two designs look similar in a brochure ("wellness rewards up to 30%") but have **opposite consequences for exit freedom.**
> **New generalisable check:** *"Classify the wellness reward: is it an **accrued wallet/balance** (forfeited on exit → lock-in, weight against portability) or a **per-renewal discount** (nothing to forfeit → no lock-in)? Same headline %, opposite effect on your freedom to leave. A discount-structured reward is strictly better for a buyer who values the option to port."*
