## Verdict

**SBI General Super Health scores ≈3.98/5 for an individual, age 26, metro tier-1 — a strong, well-rounded plan whose greatest strengths are its contract terms and its parent, and whose one real weakness is a metro cashless network that is thinner than its "largest network" billing suggests.**

**Buy at: Platinum, ₹25L (₹22,727/yr) or ₹50L (₹25,895/yr)** — office premium = final price (GST 0%); ≈₹19,090 at ₹25L after Direct-10% + long-term-6% discounts.

**Why it's good (the wording backs the brochure — rare):** best-in-study waiting periods (**PED 24 mo, specific-disease 12 mo, no hidden third tier** — M2 4.5); **uncapped room + ICU at actuals on every rung, Claims Shield consumables, unlimited ReInsure** (M1); a **claim-blind, zone-agnostic, age-banded premium with no escalator and a 9-year flat window from 26** (M4); an **[ICRA]AAA insurer 73.9%-owned by SBI** (M5); and **clean longevity terms that clear the override veto with no wellness-wallet lock-in** (M6).

**What to accept going in:**
- 🚩 **Network is broad but thin in metros** — 434 cashless hospitals in Mumbai vs HDFC's 520; "largest network" is a national-average artefact (M3). *Verify your own pin code is well-covered before buying.*
- ⚠️ **The insurer loses money underwriting** (109.6% combined ratio) → **budget for block re-pricing** over the hold (M4/M5); renewability guarantees access, not price (M6).
- ⚠️ **Buy the big SI at entry** — enhancing later restarts PED/specific/moratorium clocks on the increase (M2/M6); ₹50L is only +14% over ₹25L (M4). *But ₹30L+ triggers mandatory medicals that ₹25L avoids (M4).*
- ⚠️ **Pay annually** (grace period gives no cover for annual payers; instalments trigger the claim-deduction trap), **renew on time**, and **over-disclose at proposal** (non-disclosure = void + forfeit, until the 5-yr moratorium).
