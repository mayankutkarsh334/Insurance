> **Read this first — plain-English framing**
> The premium is the yearly price. Two things make Activ One MAX's *real* cost unusual: **(1) HealthReturns** pays you *back* up to 100% of your premium for staying active, and **(2) since 22 Sept 2025 there is 0% GST** on individual health insurance — the 18% tax that used to sit on top is gone. So the "sticker" is lower than a year ago, and an active 26-year-old can push the *net* cost far below even that.

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **HealthReturns** | Cash you earn (a % of premium) for being active — spend it on next year's premium or medical bills |
| **Age-banded premium** | Price jumps only when you cross into a new age *band* (e.g. 5-yr blocks), not every single year — gentler & predictable |
| **Zone-based pricing** | Your city's price band; metros (Delhi/Mumbai = Zone 1) cost the most |
| **Deductible** | An amount you agree to pay first each claim, in return for a cheaper premium |
| **GST** | The government tax on the premium — **now 0%** for individual health cover (was 18%) |
| **Section 80D** | Tax rule: your health premium is subtracted from taxable income, saving you income tax |
| **Block repricing** | The insurer raising the price for the *whole* product cohort — the real long-term cost threat |

## Premium dynamics (the levers)
| Lever | Detail (plain-English) |
|-------|------------------------|
| **Claims-experience discount (premium ↑/↓ with claims)** | **None / not applicable** — ABHI does **not** run a favourable-claims-experience pricing swing, and **claim-based renewal loading is banned** (E.2, IRDAI). Your claim history does *not* raise your renewal price. HealthReturns (below) is the earn mechanism instead |
| **Underwriting risk loadings (max %)** | Declared health conditions can trigger a premium **loading, extra waiting, or permanent exclusion** via counter-offer (E.2.4) — applied at entry and on SI increase. A healthy 26-yo → usually **none**. (No published hard cap like HDFC's 100%/150%) |
| **Deductible-linked discount** | Optional **Per Claim Deductible ₹15,000 / ₹25,000** (C.13.4) cuts the premium; trade-off = you pay that slice on *every* claim. Opt-in at inception only |
| **Discount stack** | **Long-term (multi-year) pre-payment: 7.5% for a 2-yr term, 10% for a 3-yr term** paid upfront · family/floater discount · **PPN 10% discount** (but adds a 10% co-pay if you use a non-preferred hospital) · online/digital where offered |
| **Instalment-premium option** | Half-yearly / quarterly / monthly available (E.1.17, auto-debit/ECS) — ⚠️ **carries a claim trap, see below** |
| **Wellness earn-back (net premium)** | ⭐ **HealthReturns — up to 100% of premium back** (C.12.2). Earned via **Healthy Heart Score** (Green tier) + **Active Dayz** (10,000 steps / 300 cal / gym session per day): **30% monthly (Green + 13 days/mo) + 20% (275 Active Dayz/yr) + 50% (325 Active Dayz/yr) = 100%.** Spend on renewal premium, bills, deductible, OPD, wearable. **Unique vs HDFC (has none)** — for an active 26-yo, a huge *net*-cost cut |
| **Section 80D effective cost** | Premium is **fully deductible up to ₹25,000/yr** (self, < 60). With **0% GST**, the whole premium counts. At a 30% tax slab that's **up to ₹7,500/yr back**. (SAVR variant adds parents, up to ₹50k.) *Note: 80D applies under the old tax regime; nil/limited under the new regime — check your regime* |
| **Zone-based pricing / zone co-pay** | **3 zones** (Zone 1 Delhi-NCR/Mumbai/Gujarat = priciest; Zone 2 metros; Zone 3 rest). Metro Tier-1 buyer pays Zone 1. **No zone co-pay** (M2) — zones affect *price only* |
| **GST on premium** | **0% since 22 Sept 2025** (was 18%) for individual/family-floater health cover. **You now pay the base premium only** — a ~15% real drop vs a year ago. *(Group/employer policies still 18%.)* **Framework update — see Rule 3** |
| **Age-banded vs annual increase** | ✅ **Age-banded** — premium steps up only at band boundaries, **not every year** — gentler and more predictable than annual-increase plans |
| **Medical-inflation / block-repricing risk** | The real long-term threat: the insurer repricing the *whole* cohort (E.1.4 allows revision with **30-day notice**, subject to Product Management Committee/IRDAI). UIN `...V022627` = **v02, a newer product (~2024)** → little revision history yet. **Multi-year pre-payment (2–3 yr) locks the rate for the term** — a partial inflation hedge |
| **Base-vs-(base+super top-up) cost** | ABHI also sells a **Super Health / Activ Health super top-up**; a moderate MAX base + super top-up can reach very high cover cheaper than one giant base — but note MAX's **inbuilt Super Credit already inflates the pot to 6×/₹3Cr for free**, reducing the need for a separate top-up (see Coverage Architecture) |
| **Premium revision history** | Newer product (v02) — limited public history; frequency *unverified* (would need AB's filed revision notices to confirm) |

## How your real cost is built
```
   Base rate (AGE band + SI)  ×  ZONE (Delhi = highest)
          ▼   + 0% GST   ◄── the 18% tax is GONE since Sept 2025
     = STICKER PREMIUM
          ▼   − Long-term discount (7.5%/2yr, 10%/3yr)
          ▼   − HealthReturns earned (up to 100% for an active, healthy year)
          ▼   − Section 80D tax saved (up to ₹7,500 at 30% slab)
     = REAL COST   ◄── can be a small fraction of sticker for an active 26-yo
```

**Worked example (indicative, ₹25L @26, Zone 1):** sticker ~₹12,500 → −10% long-term ≈ ₹11,250 → an active year earning even 50% HealthReturns ≈ **~₹5,600 net** → minus ~₹1,700 of 80D relief (30% slab) → **effective ~₹3,900 for ₹25L of cover**. Even a modest-activity year (say 20% HealthReturns) lands near ~₹8,300 net. *(HealthReturns is effort/health-score dependent — not guaranteed.)*

## ⚠️ Instalment-premium claim trap (from wording E.1.17)
If you pay monthly/quarterly and then make a claim, **all remaining instalments of the year immediately fall due, and AB can deduct them from your claim payout** (E.1.17 f & g). So the "affordable EMI" quietly reduces your payout by the unpaid balance. Prefer **annual payment** if cash-flow allows. *(Feeds M6.)*

## Projected lifetime outgo (indicative)
- **10-yr (26→35):** the cheapest decade — age-banded so it barely moves early. Nominal sticker ~₹1.2–1.4L total at ₹25L; **HealthReturns + 0% GST can cut the net to well under half** for an active holder.
- **20-yr (26→45):** still moderate — the steep climb is after 50. Net outgo materially below sticker if HealthReturns is earned consistently; multi-year pre-payment hedges some repricing.

## Module 4 score: **5 / 5**
**Rationale:** Value is Activ One's whole thesis, and MAX delivers it: a competitive sticker (**~₹10,149 @25 for ₹15L, Zone 1, now GST-free**), **sub-linear SI pricing** (higher cover is cheap per rupee), **age-banded increases** (gentle, predictable), **long-term pre-payment discounts (up to 10%)**, and above all **HealthReturns returning up to 100% of premium** — an earn-back HDFC simply doesn't have. Layered with **0% GST** and **80D relief**, the *net* cost for an active, healthy 26-year-old is very low. Held at 5 despite two real watch-outs: the **instalment-claim trap** (E.1.17, feeds M6) and the fact that **HealthReturns is effort/health-score-dependent, not guaranteed** — the sticker alone is merely competitive, not the cheapest.
