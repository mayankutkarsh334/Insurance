## Key findings by module

**M1 (Coverage & benefits):**
- **MAX makes Claim Protect (consumables) and Super Credit (growth bonus) inbuilt** — the two features that were paid options in NXT — matching the HDFC benchmark.
- **No room cap / no sub-limits at any SI tier**, modern treatments + mental illness + HIV up to SI, **90-day pre-hosp (beats HDFC's 60)**, unlimited Super Reload, SI to ₹6Cr, and **HealthReturns up to 100% of premium back**.
- ⚠️ **Super Credit "6×" is hard-capped at ₹3Cr** — fully delivered only up to ~₹60L base; clipped at ₹1Cr SI.
- ⚠️ **UIN discrepancy** between CIS (ADIHLIP24097V012324) and wording/brochure (ADIHLIP27048V022627) — confirm on the Policy Schedule.

**M2 (Exclusions & waiting):**
- **No mandatory co-pay, no sub-limits, no modern-treatment caps, true mental-illness parity, no zonal co-pay** — a clean contract; PED wait 3 yr but **individually reducible** (better than HDFC's channel-only cut); optional **Day-1 chronic waiver** for 7 conditions.
- ⚠️ **Waiting-reduction options are SI-gated** (new finding): PED cut offered only ≤ ₹50L, specific-disease cut only ≤ ₹25L — a **₹1Cr buyer cannot shorten any waiting period**.
- Specific-disease wait is **2 years** (a year longer than some rivals); maternity fully excluded in MAX (VIP+ only).

**M3 (Claims & servicing):** _(insurer-level — same for any Activ One variant)_
- **CSR ~95.9% (3-yr 95.81%, above ~91% industry), 100% of claims settled within 3 months, complaints 18.67/10k (below avg)**, in-house claims, contractual **1-hour pre-auth**, 15-day settlement + penal interest, **network density ~15.8/district (rank #2)**.
- ⚠️ **ICR 68.16% vs HDFC 84.85%** — AB pays a smaller share of each premium rupee; **by-amount payout undisclosed** (watch-point, not a red flag).

**M4 (Cost & premium):**
- **Sourced anchor: ₹10,149/yr @ age 25, ₹15L, Delhi (Zone 1), GST-free.** Age-banded (gentle steps), **sub-linear SI pricing** (higher cover cheap per rupee), long-term pre-pay discounts (7.5% 2-yr / 10% 3-yr).
- ⭐ **HealthReturns up to 100% of premium back** + **0% GST since 22 Sept 2025** + 80D → net cost for an active 26-yo is very low.
- ⚠️ **Instalment-claim trap** (E.1.17): claim makes all remaining instalments fall due and be deducted from the payout — prefer annual payment (feeds M6).

**M5 (Insurer quality):** _(company-level — same for any Activ One variant)_
- **Aditya Birla group backing**, solvency **1.98 (Mar 2025, rising)**, clean record, fast growth (**GWP ₹4,940cr +33%, 12.6% SAHI share, 24M+ lives**).
- ⚠️ **Younger insurer (~9–10 yrs vs HDFC ~24)**, **still underwriting-loss-making (combined ratio 114%, improving from 121%)**, and **health-entity credit rating unverified** (the AAA is Aditya Birla *Sun Life*, a different company).

**M6 (Fine print & longevity):** _(wording-level — same for any Activ One variant)_
- **Lifelong renewal, no max entry age, 30-day grace & free-look, 60-mo moratorium, portability/migration with full continuity, no claim-based loading** — clean, IRDAI-compliant, reader-friendly.
- ⚠️ Frictions HDFC lacks: **HealthReturns lock-in** (accrued earn-back forfeited on switching — now confirmed from the wording), **an arbitration clause that is a condition precedent to suit**, and **monoline structure with no cross-subsidy cushion**. **No override veto.**

### 🔄 January 2026 re-test — what changed, and what didn't

MAX was studied **third**, so ~15 dimensions discovered later (in SBI, Care Supreme and ACKO) had never been applied to it. All six modules were re-tested. **No score changed** — the new findings cut both ways and netted out.

**⭐ What the re-test CONFIRMED as best-in-study:**
- **Wording-fixed vs Schedule-delegated limits (ACKO M1):** only **3 schedule delegations in 56 pages** — versus HDFC ~6 and **ACKO 37**. **The strongest contractual guarantee of any plan studied**: "no room cap" here is a promise, not a setting.
- **Zero voluntary modifiers:** no opt-in room cap (HDFC has one), no network-gatekeeping or notification-linked co-pay (Care has two, ACKO four).
- **100% of claims settled within 3 months** — the best in the study — and a **computed ~4.1% repudiation rate**, roughly half the industry's ~8%. **MAX is the only plan whose repudiation rate can be computed from disclosed data.**
- **ICR ~71.5% sits ABOVE its SAHI peer benchmark (68.06%)** — only MAX and HDFC clear their own segment.
- **Day-1 chronic cover** (diabetes/hypertension/asthma) — **unique in the study**.
- **No tobacco/lifestyle-attributable exclusion** — Care and ACKO both carry one.
- **Mental-illness hospitalisation is an expressly named covered benefit** — where **HDFC and ACKO have zero mentions of mental illness anywhere in their wordings**.
- **No instalment claim trap** — a check **HDFC and Care both fail**.

**🚩 What the re-test newly found AGAINST it:**
- **An ARBITRATION clause drafted as a condition precedent to suit** (M6) — *"it shall be a condition precedent to any right of action or suit… that the award… be first obtained"*, and **asymmetric**: it binds you on *quantum* disputes but not where the insurer denies liability outright. **Only ACKO and MAX carry this**; HDFC, SBI, Care and Bajaj do not.
- **HealthReturns is a forfeitable WALLET, confirmed from the wording** (M6) — *"earning on HealthReturns shall automatically lapse"* on termination. **The study's richest earn-back (up to 100% of premium) is therefore also its strongest lock-in**, since the balance is lost if you port.
- **MAX is a MONOLINE SAHI** (M5) — like Care Supreme it has **no other line to cross-subsidise a bad health year**, so only two fixes exist (reprice or pay less) and both land on the policyholder. ✅ **What separates it from Care: its combined ratio is *improving* (~121% → ~114%) and the Aditya Birla Group is a far deeper parent.**
- **Grace-period coverage gap** — cover does not run during the 30 days for annual payers (instalment payers are covered). Universal across the finalists.

**⚠️ Open items to close before deciding:** MAX's **metro cashless-hospital count** (never tested against the same source used for HDFC 520 / SBI 434 / Care 299); **core solvency ex-sub-debt**; the **claims-vs-expense split** of its combined ratio; its **current credit rating**; and whether late claim intimation is a **condition precedent**.

The shortlisted **MAX** variant closes the two gaps that held the NXT study at 4.35: **consumables (Claim Protect) and the growth bonus (Super Credit) are now inbuilt** — so coverage (M1 = 5) and exclusions (M2 = 5) score full marks, and **value (M4 = 5)** is the standout, powered by **HealthReturns (up to 100% of premium back)**, the new **0% GST**, age-banded pricing and sub-linear SI cost. It trails HDFC Optima Secure+ by a hair on the two insurer-level modules: **claims (M3 = 4** — lower ICR 68% and undisclosed by-amount payout) and **insurer strength (M5 = 4** — younger, still underwriting-loss-making, unverified health-entity rating). Fine print (M6 = 4) is clean with two soft frictions (HealthReturns lock-in, no instalment refund).

**Best fit:** an **active, healthy 26-year-old** who will actually earn HealthReturns and hold the policy long-term, ideally at a **₹25–50L base** (the value sweet spot where Super Credit isn't clipped, waiting-reductions are still offered, and premium-per-rupee is lowest). **Pay annually** (not in instalments) to sidestep the claim-deduction and no-refund traps. **Before buying:** confirm the Policy Schedule names the **MAX** variant and quotes the current **UIN (…V022627)**.
