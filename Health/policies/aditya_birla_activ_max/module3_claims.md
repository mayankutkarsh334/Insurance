# Module 3 — Claims & Servicing

_Source: **IRDAI FY2024-25 disclosures** + insurer/aggregator data + **binding wording** `resources/Policy Document_MAX.pdf` (Section E.2.7 Claims, E.1.9 Settlement). **Claims metrics are insurer-level (Aditya Birla Health Insurance Co. Ltd) — identical for the MAX, NXT or any Activ One variant.** UIN ADIHLIP27048V022627._

> **Read this first — plain-English framing**
> This is the **"will they actually pay, and quickly?"** module. A feature-rich, cheap plan is worthless if the company disputes bills or drags its feet. We check four things: (1) **how much** they pay out, (2) **how often** people complain, (3) **how big/dense** the cashless hospital network is, and (4) **how fast** the contract forces settlement. This is the highest-weighted module in the whole study (**25%**).

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **CSR (Claim Settlement Ratio)** | Of all claims, the % they said "yes" to — counted by *number of claims* |
| **ICR (Incurred Claim Ratio)** | Of every ₹100 of premium collected, how much came back to customers as claims. Too low = stingy/very profitable; ~75–90% = healthy; >100% = losing money |
| **Cashless** | The hospital bills the insurer directly — you don't pay upfront and wait for a refund |
| **Pre-authorisation** | The insurer's "yes, we'll pay" approval *before* cashless treatment |
| **In-house vs TPA** | In-house = the insurer's own staff decide claims (usually faster, one less middleman); TPA = an outsourced administrator |
| **Network density** | Cashless hospitals **per district** — a fairer measure than a big raw national number |
| **TAT (Turn-Around Time)** | How long they take to settle |

### The "by count vs by amount" gap — why 95.88% isn't the whole story
```
  CSR (by count) = 95.88%   →  "96 out of 100 claims got a YES"   ✅ looks great
                                  BUT says nothing about HOW MUCH of each bill was paid

  A claim can be "settled" yet trimmed: R&C cuts, non-payable items, sub-limits.
  The only public clue to "how much of my bill" is the ICR:

     HDFC  ICR 84.85%  ──►  ₹85 paid per ₹100 premium
     AB    ICR 68.16%  ──►  ₹68 paid per ₹100 premium   ← retains ₹17 more per ₹100
```
This does **not** mean AB underpays individual bills — a low ICR on a **fast-growing book** (lots of new, young, healthy policyholders who haven't claimed yet) is normal and even a sign of pricing discipline. But it's a **watch-point**, not a strength, and the by-amount CSR would settle it. *(This is the HDFC-M3 "payout by count vs amount" lens applied.)*

## Claim mechanics (from the binding wording)

| Item | Detail | Verdict |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Cashless pre-auth deadline** | **Planned:** contact ≥ 72 h before treatment advisable; **Emergency:** within **24 h** of hospitalisation | standard |
| **Pre-auth decision speed** | Authorisation letter issued to the hospital **"immediately, but not more than ONE HOUR of receiving complete information"** (E.2.7.1.b.iv & c.v) | ✅ **Meets the IRDAI 1-hour mandate in the contract itself** |
| **Reimbursement intimation window** | Non-network / non-cashless: written notice within **48 h of admission or before discharge**, whichever earlier (delay condoned on merit) | standard |
| **Document submission window** | Within **30 days of discharge**; pre/post-hospitalisation docs within **30 days of completing post-hosp treatment** | standard |
| **Settlement TAT** | **Within 15 days** of claim submission (E.1.9) | ✅ matches IRDAI floor |
| **Penal interest for delay** | **2% above the RBI bank rate**, from date of claim intimation to date of payment (E.1.9) | ✅ mandated protection present |
| **TPA vs in-house adjudication** | **In-house claims model** (standalone health insurer / SAHI) — insurer-stated; wording still *permits* a TPA ("Us, TPA or jointly"). TPA involvement not fully ruled out — *verify on your policy schedule* | generally a plus (fewer middlemen) |
| **IRDAI 2024 mandate delivery** (cashless-anywhere, 1-hr pre-auth, 3-hr discharge) | **1-hr pre-auth: in the wording ✅.** Cashless-anywhere: participates in "Cashless Everywhere". **3-hr discharge: not written into the wording** (it's a regulatory mandate) — real delivery corroborated indirectly by **100%-within-3-months settlement + below-average complaints** | mostly good; 3-hr discharge = trust-but-verify |
| **Cashless outside India / 100% cashless** | **Global cashless only under the VIP+ variant** — **not** available in MAX. MAX is domestic-cashless | note if you want worldwide cover |
| **Condition Precedent / moratorium protection** | Claim notification is a "Condition Precedent" (miss it and delay must be justified). After **60 months** no claim is contestable except proven fraud | standard + moratorium safety net (feeds M6) |

## SI-scaling read — how claims/servicing change across ₹10L / 25L / 50L / 1Cr
- **Everything in this module is insurer-level, so it is identical at every SI tier.** ICR 68%, CSR ~96%, 100%-within-3-months, the network, the 1-hr pre-auth and 15-day settlement do **not** change whether you buy ₹10L or ₹1Cr.
- The only faint SI linkage: a **larger claim (high SI)** is more likely to be **investigated** (the wording lets AB investigate within 15 days) and to attract **R&C / non-payable scrutiny** — but that's true of every insurer, not an AB-specific SI effect.

## New framework dimension surfaced (Rule 3)
- **Settlement-speed distribution (TAT buckets), not just CSR** — IRDAI's disclosure of *what % of claims are paid within 3 months / 3–6 months / 6–12 months / >1 year* is a sharper "will they pay *quickly*" signal than a single CSR%. AB scores **100% within 3 months**, which is more reassuring than its CSR alone. Recommend adding to `study_plan.md` M3 as a check: *"Pull the IRDAI claims-paid-by-ageing-bucket table (% settled <3 mo / 3–6 mo / 6–12 mo / >1 yr) — a plan can have a high CSR yet a slow tail."* Applies to all insurers. **(Flagged for addition — see chat summary + study_plan edit.)**

## Module 3 score: **4 / 5**
**Rationale:** Genuinely strong, health-focused servicing: **CSR ~95.9% (3-yr 95.81%, above the ~91% industry avg)**, **100% of claims settled within 3 months**, **below-average complaints (18.67/10k)**, in-house claims, a contractual **1-hour pre-auth**, 15-day settlement with penal interest, and **rank-#2 network density (~15.8/district)**. It sits **one notch below the HDFC benchmark (5/5)** on two counts: the **lower ICR (68% vs 85%)** means AB pays a smaller share of each premium rupee (efficient-growing-book vs stingy is ambiguous without by-amount CSR), and **by-amount payout is undisclosed**. Comfortably good, not best-in-class.
