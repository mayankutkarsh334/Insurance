## Plain-English framing (read this first)

"Coverage" = **the list of things the policy will pay for, and how big the money-pot is.** A health policy has one main pot (your **Sum Insured**) plus some "top-up" pots that refill or grow it. This module answers: *when you're in hospital, what does Activ One MAX actually pay, and where are the silent holes?*

| Term | In one plain sentence |
|------|-----------------------|
| **Sum Insured (SI)** | The yearly pot of money the insurer will pay your hospital bills from. |
| **Room rent "at actuals"** | No cap on your hospital room's daily cost — so you avoid the nasty "proportionate deduction" trap (explained below). |
| **Claim Protect** | Pays the ~100 small "non-medical" items (gloves, masks, syringes, gowns) that insurers normally make *you* pay — usually 10–20% of a bill. |
| **Super Reload** | If you empty your pot mid-year, it **refills to full** so the next illness is still covered. |
| **Super Credit** | A **free yearly top-up** that grows your pot bigger every year even if you never claim. |
| **HealthReturns** | **Cash-back you earn** for staying fit — up to 100% of your premium, usable to pay renewals or bills. |
| **Waiting period** | A starting delay before certain illnesses are covered (dealt with fully in Module 2). |

### The "proportionate deduction" trap — why "room at actuals" matters
Many cheap policies cap your room rent (say "1% of SI/day"). If you then take a costlier room, the insurer scales down **your entire bill** by the same ratio — not just the room.

```
   CAPPED plan (room cap ₹5,000/day, you take a ₹10,000 room):
   Total bill ₹4,00,000  ──►  insurer pays only 50%  ──►  you lose ₹2,00,000

   ACTIV ONE MAX (room "at actuals", no cap):
   Total bill ₹4,00,000  ──►  insurer pays 100%      ──►  you lose ₹0
```
Activ One MAX has **no room cap by default at every SI tier** — the trap is switched off.

## Benefit-stacking math — how the pot grows (Base SI = **B**)

Think of it as **four stacked buckets** that fill in a fixed order:

```
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  4. CANCER BOOSTER   (optional add-on, cancer only)          │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │  3. SUPER RELOAD     refills a fresh "B" — UNLIMITED times/yr │  ← catastrophe catcher
  │                      (kicks in from the 2nd claim onwards)    │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │  2. SUPER CREDIT     +100% of B each year, up to 500% (6×),   │  ← grows even if you never claim
  │                      hard cap ₹3 Cr  [INBUILT in MAX]         │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │  1. BASE SUM INSURED (B)   room/ICU/everything at actuals     │  ← the pot you buy
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
     Money is spent bottom-up: Base → Super Credit → Super Reload → Cancer Booster
```

| Layer | Activ One MAX | vs HDFC Optima Secure+ benchmark |
|-------|---------------|----------------------------------|
| Base SI (B) | Room + ICU + all listed treatments **at actuals, no cap**, up to B | Same (both no cap) |
| Instant multiplier | *(No day-1 2× multiplier in Activ One — it grows via Super Credit instead)* | HDFC gives 2× day-1 "Secure"; ABHI's is a year-by-year build, so **HDFC has more cover in year 1** |
| Annual bonus (**Super Credit**) | **Inbuilt in MAX**: +100% of B per year → up to **500% of B (6× by year 6)**, **irrespective of claims**, **hard cap ₹3 Cr** | HDFC "Infinite" is inbuilt **and uncapped** — MAX's ₹3Cr cap bites at high SI (see below) |
| Restoration (**Super Reload**) | **Inbuilt** — refills a full B, **unlimited times/year**, but **only from the 2nd claim in the policy's life** | HDFC restores **from the 1st claim** — small edge to HDFC |
| Cancer Booster | Optional add-on (extra pot for cancer, up to 100% of B) | — |
| **Per-claim cap rule** | A single claim draws Base → Super Credit → Super Reload in sequence; **one Super Reload event ≤ Base SI** | Same shape as HDFC |

### The ₹3 Cr Super-Credit cap — an SI-scaling nuance *(finding)*
Super Credit promises "6× your Base SI," but there's a **hard ₹3 Cr ceiling on this benefit**. That means the "6×" is only fully delivered up to a Base SI of ~₹60L:

| Base SI | Full 6× would be | Actually capped at | Effective multiplier |
|--------:|-----------------:|-------------------:|:--------------------:|
| ₹10 L | ₹60 L | ₹60 L | full 6× ✅ |
| ₹25 L | ₹1.5 Cr | ₹1.5 Cr | full 6× ✅ |
| ₹50 L | ₹3 Cr | ₹3 Cr | full 6× ✅ (just at the line) |
| ₹1 Cr | ₹6 Cr | **₹3 Cr** | **only ~4× (3× credit)** ❌ clipped |

**Takeaway:** the "grows 6×" story is fully true up to ₹50L SI; at ₹1Cr it's clipped. For a 26-year-old, a **₹25L–₹50L** base already reaches a huge multi-crore pot without hitting the cap.

## HealthReturns — the earn-back, in plain numbers *(feeds Module 4)*

A 26-year-old who stays active can earn back **up to 100% of premium** — effectively becoming premium-free. Two independent grids stack:

```
  MONTHLY  (based on fitness "Healthy Heart Score" + Active Dayz)
    Green score + 13 active days/month ......... 30% of premium
    Amber ...................................... up to 12%
    Red ....................................... up to 6%

  ANNUAL  bonus slabs (on top of monthly)
    275 Active Dayz in the year ............... +20%
    325 Active Dayz in the year ............... +50%

  BEST CASE (Green + 13 days/month + 325 days/yr) = 30 + 20 + 50 = 100% back
```
- **1 Active Day** = 10,000 steps (age < 60), OR 30 min gym/yoga, OR 300 calories burnt, OR a marathon/walkathon.
- Spendable on: renewal premium, bills once SI exhausted, deductible, OPD, non-payables, a fitness wearable.
- ⚠️ Down-weight caution (M6): the wallet is **forfeited if you port/cancel** — a mild lock-in. Weigh only the *net* premium it delivers, not the headline "100%".

## SI-scaling read — how coverage changes across ₹10L / 25L / 50L / 1Cr

**The core design is size-independent:** almost everything is *"at actuals up to Sum Insured"*, so the main covers (room, ICU, treatments, ambulance, modern procedures, AYUSH, consumables) **scale linearly** — no room cap or sub-limit appears at the low ₹10L tier that vanishes at ₹1Cr. This is the same clean shape as the HDFC benchmark.

Two things *do* scale non-linearly:

| Item | ₹10 L | ₹25 L | ₹50 L | ₹1 Cr | Comment |
|------|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|---------|
| **Super Credit 6× ceiling** | full ₹60L | full ₹1.5Cr | full ₹3Cr | **clipped at ₹3Cr (~4×)** | ₹3Cr hard cap bites only at ₹1Cr+ |
| **Health check-up panel** | basic panel | mid panel | **richest panel** (X-ray, USG, HbA1c…) | richest | better diagnostics at higher SI |
| **Flat-rupee optional caps** (DME ₹5L, compassionate ₹50k, cancer screen ₹10k) | proportionally **larger** vs SI | — | — | small at ₹1Cr | these caps pinch more at low SI |

**Bottom line for a 26-yo:** a **₹25L–₹50L** base is the sweet spot — full 6× Super Credit (multi-crore pot), richest check-up, no cap traps. ₹1Cr works but partly wastes the Super Credit promise.

## New framework dimension surfaced (Rule 3)
- **Benefit hard-caps that clip a headline multiplier at high SI** — the Super Credit "6×" is truncated by a flat ₹3Cr ceiling above ~₹60L base. This is distinct from a room/disease sub-limit; it's a *bonus-benefit* cap that quietly changes the value proposition as SI grows. Recommend adding to `study_plan.md` M1 as a check: *"Do any 'multiplier' benefits (day-1 X, infinite bonus, restore) carry a flat-rupee ceiling that clips them at higher SI?"* — applies to all plans with a growing-bonus feature. **(Flagged for addition; see chat summary.)**
