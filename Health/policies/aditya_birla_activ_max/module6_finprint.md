# Module 6 — Fine Print & Longevity

_Source: **binding policy wording** `resources/Policy Document_MAX.pdf` — Section E (General Terms & Conditions) + IRDAI norms. UIN **ADIHLIP27048V022627**. The wording helpfully includes plain-English "Simpler version" boxes. **Fine print is shared across all Activ One variants**, so this matches the NXT read; confirmed here against the MAX document._

> **Read this first — plain-English framing**
> This is the contract's *small print* — the clauses that decide whether the insurer can drop you, deny a late claim, short-change a refund, or quietly re-price you years from now. For a policy you intend to hold for **decades**, these matter as much as the benefits. Good news: this wording is unusually reader-friendly and IRDAI-compliant. The two real frictions are a **wellness-wallet lock-in** and **no refund for instalment payers**.

## Plain-English glossary
| Term | In simple words |
|------|-----------------|
| **Lifelong renewability** | They must let you renew **for life** — can't drop you for getting older or for claiming |
| **Moratorium** | After **5 years** your cover becomes "unquestionable" — they can't dig up a non-disclosure to deny a claim (except proven fraud) |
| **Grace period** | Extra days to pay a late renewal without losing your served waiting periods |
| **Free-look** | A 30-day "try it and return it for a refund" window after buying |
| **Portability / Migration** | Your right to switch insurer (port) or switch product (migrate) **keeping** the waiting periods you've already served |
| **Lock-in** | Anything that makes leaving costly — here, **losing your earned HealthReturns** |

| **Lifelong renewability** | **Yes** (E.1.3) — "renewable... except on established fraud/non-disclosure/misrepresentation"; **renewal not denied for claiming**; no max entry age | ✅ core box ticked — *but see withdrawal caveat below* |
| **Entry max-age limit** | **None** — can be bought at any age (PBT: "Maximum – No capping") | ✅ favourable |
| **Grace period** | **30 days** (annual/half-yearly/quarterly); **15 days** for monthly. Continuity preserved if paid within grace; **no cover during the grace gap** | ✅ standard |
| **Claim-based premium loading** | **Banned** (E.1.3 vi: "No loading shall apply on renewals based on individual claims experience") | ✅ claiming never raises *your* price |
| **Non-disclosure remedies** | Declared conditions can be excluded/loaded via counter-offer (M2); misrepresentation/non-disclosure/fraud → cancellation with **no refund**. **Honest best-of-knowledge mistakes are protected** (E.1.6: fraud clause doesn't bite if the misstatement was true to the best of your knowledge) | be truthful on the proposal form |
| **Condition Precedent / Complete Discharge / Fraud** | Claim notification & compliance are **Conditions Precedent** to liability (E.2.7); **fraud forfeits all benefits + premium** and voids the policy (E.1.6) | standard |
| **Multiple policies / coordination of benefits** | You **choose which policy to claim first**; if one policy's SI is short, the insurer settles its share and you can recover the balance from the other (E.1) | ✅ helpful if you also hold a top-up/corporate cover |
| **Free-look period** | **30 days** on new individual policies (not on renewals/ports); refund of premium (less medical-exam/stamp/proportionate-risk costs) within **7 days** | ✅ standard |
| **Cancellation & refund** | **You:** 7-day notice → **proportionate refund of the unexpired period — but only if no claim, and only for Yearly/Annual/One-Time premium.** ⚠️ **No refund at all for Half-yearly / Quarterly / Monthly frequencies** (E.1.2). **No refund once any claim is admitted/lodged/benefit availed.** **Insurer:** cancel for misrepresentation/fraud with 15-day notice, no refund | ⚠️ instalment payers get **nothing** back on cancellation |
| **Earn-back / wellness-wallet forfeiture (lock-in)** | ⚠️ **Yes** — on cancellation, all coverage, benefits **and HealthReturns lapse**; only *unclaimed, already-accrued* HealthReturns can be claimed within a short window (~3 months) after cancellation (E.1) | the more you bank in HealthReturns, the **costlier to leave** — a soft lock-in, and it bites harder because this is a young, repricing-prone product |
| **Instalment-premium claim trap** | ⚠️ **Yes** — claim while paying in instalments → **all remaining instalments of the year fall due at once and are deducted from your payout** (E.1.17 f/g; E.1.2 note) | prefer **annual** payment (feeds M4) |

| **Grievance → Ombudsman → IRDAI path** | AB grievance cell → Grievance Officer (gro.healthinsurance@...) → **Insurance Ombudsman** → **IRDAI Bima Bharosa (IGMS)** | ✅ clear, standard ladder |

## The two real frictions — visualised
```
  FRICTION 1 — HealthReturns lock-in
     You earn ₹₹₹ HealthReturns over years for staying active ...
     ... then port/cancel  ──►  the wallet is FORFEITED (only unclaimed accrued, claimable ~3 mo)
     ⇒ the more value you bank, the more you're "glued" to AB even if it hikes prices

  FRICTION 2 — Instalment payers get no exit value
     Pay monthly/quarterly ──► cancel ──► ₹0 refund
                            ──► OR claim ──► remaining instalments deducted from payout
     ⇒ "affordable EMI" quietly removes flexibility. Annual payment avoids both.
```
Neither is a dealbreaker for a healthy 26-year-old planning to *hold* the policy — but both make **leaving or paying-in-bits** more costly than they look.

## SI-scaling read
- Fine-print rights are **identical across SI tiers** (₹10L or ₹1Cr — same contract).
- Same **enhancement-of-SI trap** as every plan: raising SI later **restarts the moratorium and all waiting periods on the increase** — so a larger base up front (within the ₹25–50L value sweet spot from M1/M2/M4) avoids re-serving clocks on a future top-up.

## New framework dimension surfaced (Rule 3)
- **"Lifelong renewal" is conditional on the *product* surviving — withdrawal-and-migrate re-prices you.** The wording guarantees renewal *"provided the product is not withdrawn"* (E.1.3) and, on withdrawal, migration to a *similar* product **with continuity of SI/waiting/bonus — but NOT of the premium rate**. So "renewable for life" protects your *continuity*, not your *price*: a young, v02, still-loss-making product (see M5) carries a higher-than-average chance of being withdrawn/replaced, and the replacement can be re-priced. Recommend adding to `study_plan.md` M6 as: *"Distinguish guaranteed **renewability** (continuity) from guaranteed **pricing** — a product-withdrawal + forced-migration can preserve waiting-period credit while resetting the premium; weight this higher for young/loss-making products."* Applies to all plans. **(Flagged for addition — see chat summary + study_plan edit.)**
