Product "Activ One" (**MAX** variant), UIN **ADIHLIP27048V022627**. Wording governs._

## Plain-English framing (read this first)

- **Waiting period** = a starting delay. You hold the policy, but certain illnesses aren't covered **until the clock runs out**. This is where a huge share of early claim denials come from.
- **Exclusion** = something the policy **never** pays for, no matter how long you wait.
- **Co-pay** = a slice of *every* bill that stays your problem (e.g. "10% co-pay" = you pay 10% of everything). **Zero co-pay is best.**
- **Sub-limit** = a cap on one specific treatment (e.g. "cataract max ₹40k"), separate from your main pot.

**The rule of thumb:** a *good* plan covers things **sooner** (short waits), excludes **less**, and forces **no co-pay / no sub-limits**. Activ One MAX does well on all three.

### The three waiting clocks — visualised
```
  Day 0 ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────►  time
   │
   ├─ 30 days ─────────► general illnesses covered   (accidents: covered from Day 0)
   │
   ├─ 2 years ───────────────────────► "specific diseases" list covered
   │                                     (cataract, hernia, knee, piles, gynae, etc.)
   │
   └─ 3 years ─────────────────────────────────► pre-existing diseases (PED) covered
                                                   (illnesses you already had at purchase)

  Optional (paid) accelerators, at inception only:
    • PED clock: 3 yr → 2 yr, or 3 yr → 1 yr     (offered only for SI ₹10L–₹50L)
    • Specific clock: 2 yr → 1 yr                (offered only for SI ₹10L–₹25L)
    • Chronic Care add-on: 7 named conditions → covered from DAY 1 (no wait)
```

## Claim-lever definitions (extract first)

These four definitions are the levers an insurer uses to *shrink or deny* a claim even when the treatment looks covered. Extracting them up front tells you how much discretion the insurer keeps.

| Definition | Activ One MAX | Why it bites |
|------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Pre-existing disease (lookback)** | **36 months** (Def B.1.51) — anything diagnosed, or advised/treated, in the 36 months before the first policy | Sets *what counts as "pre-existing"* and so is stuck behind the 3-yr PED wait. 36 mo is the IRDAI-standard, borrower-friendly window (same as HDFC benchmark). |
| **"Any One Illness" / relapse window** | **No explicit "Any One Illness" relapse definition** in the wording | Some plans use a relapse window (a return within X days = the *same* claim, blocking restore). Its **absence is a plus** — Super Reload restores from the 2nd claim regardless of relapse timing. |
| **Reasonable & Customary charges** | **Baked into "Medical Expenses" (Def B.1.35)** — payable only up to what "other hospitals or doctors in the same locality would have charged" | A de-facto R&C clause — lets the insurer **trim an above-market bill** to "customary" rates. Discretionary. |
| **Medically Necessary Treatment** | **Defined (Def B.1.37)** and required for every hospitalisation (C.1 Note b) | Lets the insurer **reject treatment it deems unnecessary** / beyond appropriate level of care. Standard but discretionary. |

> **Read:** ABHI keeps the two normal discretionary levers (R&C + Medically Necessary) that virtually every Indian plan has — no worse than the benchmark — and **avoids** the extra "Any One Illness" relapse trap.

## Day-1 chronic cover — the standout waiting-period feature (optional C.13.8)

For anyone with a lifestyle condition, this is genuinely valuable — it **switches off the PED and 30-day waits** for **7 named chronic conditions** from Day 1:

```
   Normally:  Diabetes/BP/etc. (if pre-existing)  ──►  wait 3 YEARS
   With Chronic Care add-on:                       ──►  covered from DAY 1
```
- **Conditions:** Diabetes, Hypertension, Asthma, Hyperlipidemia (high cholesterol), COPD, Obesity, Coronary Artery Disease (PTCA > 1 yr).
- **How you qualify (C.13.8.1–3):** declared & accepted at inception; or found at pre-policy medical; or detected via Health Assessment/check-up within 3 months of start (then **no extra premium** to switch it on).
- **Catches:** it's a **paid optional** (not inbuilt, same as NXT); if you already have the condition you *must* buy it with the applicable loading; **can't be deleted** on later renewals. Exclusions D.1.1 (PED) and D.1.3 (30-day) simply don't apply to the extent of this benefit.
- Not offered by the HDFC benchmark → a real differentiator.

## SI-scaling read — how waiting/exclusions change across ₹10L / 25L / 50L / 1Cr

The **exclusions list and the three waiting clocks are identical at every SI tier**. But — a **new finding** — the *paid options that let you shorten those clocks are gated by SI*, and they **disappear at the high tiers**:

| Waiting-reduction option (paid) | ₹10 L | ₹25 L | ₹50 L | ₹1 Cr |
|---------------------------------|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|:-----:|
| **PED wait 3 yr → 2 yr / 1 yr** (C.13.2) | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ **not offered** |
| **Specific-disease wait 2 yr → 1 yr** (C.13.1) | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ **not offered** | ❌ **not offered** |
| Co-pay / sub-limits / modern-treatment caps | none | none | none | none |

**Plain meaning:** a buyer at **₹1Cr SI cannot pay to shorten any waiting period** — they're locked to the full 3-yr PED / 2-yr specific waits. The flexibility that makes ABHI's waiting story attractive is a **low-and-mid-SI feature**. For a healthy 26-year-old this rarely matters (no PEDs to rush), but it's a genuine gap for someone with a known condition who wants a very large cover. **Sweet spot again ₹25L** (both reductions available); ₹50L still gets the PED cut.

| "Limitless — no sub-limits, every base benefit up to SI" | ✅ **Confirmed** — no sub-limits, no modern-treatment caps, no co-pay |

| PED covered "after 36 months" (CIS) vs PBT "3 years" | ✅ Consistent (36 months = 3 years) |

No hard conflict; one "sells-wider-than-it-binds" gap = the **SI-gated waiting reductions**.

## New framework dimension surfaced (Rule 3)
- **SI-gated waiting-reduction options** — the paid accelerators that shorten PED/specific waits are **only offered up to certain SI tiers** (PED cut ≤ ₹50L, specific cut ≤ ₹25L), so **the high-SI buyer loses them entirely**. This is distinct from "does a reduction exist" and from "who can opt it (channel vs individual)" — it's a *third* gate: **at what SI is the reduction even sold**. Recommend adding to `study_plan.md` M2 as a check: *"If a plan offers paid PED/specific-disease waiting reductions, confirm the SI band they're offered in — some vanish at high SI."* Applies to all plans with optional waiting cuts. **(Flagged for addition — see chat summary + study_plan edit.)**
