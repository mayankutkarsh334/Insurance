# Screening Methodology: Variables That Matter

## Context
We are selecting an **individual health policy** to hold for **decades** and renew every year.
This changes what matters compared to buying a one-year product on price alone:
- A claim may happen in year 1 or year 25 — the insurer must survive and stay fair the whole time.
- The premium you see today is not the premium you'll pay at 60 — the **age-loading curve** matters.
- The benefit that saves you is the one written in the **wording**, not the brochure.

> Ranking rule for an individual buyer: **Will they pay? > What is covered? > What can they deny? > What does it cost?**

---

## Key Variables & Why They Matter

### 1. Claims Reliability (the #1 filter)
| Metric | Why it matters | Good sign |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **Incurred Claim Ratio (ICR)** | Claims paid ÷ premium collected. The real "do they pay" number | **65–90%.** Too low = stingy; >100% = unsustainable pricing |
| **Claim repudiation / rejection rate** | % of claims rejected outright | Lower is better; watch for high rejection |
| **Complaint ratio** (IRDAI, per 10k policies) | How often customers escalate | Lower is better |
| **Cashless settlement %** | Share settled cashless vs reimbursement | Higher = smoother experience |
| **Network hospital count & quality** | Cashless only works at network hospitals | Wide network incl. your city's top hospitals |

> CSR (Claim Settlement Ratio) is reported by insurers but easy to inflate. Trust ICR + complaints + repudiation instead.

### 2. Coverage Adequacy
| Metric | Why it matters |
|--------|---------------|
| **Sum insured options** | Must offer ≥ ₹10L; ideally ₹25L–₹1Cr for an individual |
| **Room rent cap** | Capped room rent triggers **proportionate deduction** on the whole bill. **No-cap / any-room is strongly preferred** |
| **Restoration / recharge** | Refills sum insured after exhaustion — crucial for a single life with one claim wiping the SI |
| **No-Claim Bonus (NCB) & bonus ceiling** | Boosts SI for claim-free years; some plans give up to 100%+. Note the **maximum bonus cap** (e.g. 100% vs 500%) |
| **Claim impact on bonus (bonus protection)** | Does making a claim **reduce** your accrued NCB? Some plans cut bonus by 50% (or the year's %) after a claim; "bonus protect" plans don't. **Distinct from claim-based premium loading** — this erodes your *cover*, not your premium |
| **Pre/post-hospitalisation** | Days of cover before/after admission (e.g. 60/180) |
| **Day-care & modern treatments** | Robotic surgery, etc. now common; must be covered |
| **Consumables cover** | Gloves/PPE etc. are a large silent out-of-pocket unless covered |
| **Ambulance & organ-donor cover** | Ambulance is a small but universal cost; organ-donor cover pays the donor's hospitalisation in a transplant — check limit |

### 3. Exclusions & Restrictions (where claims die)
| Metric | Why it matters | Prefer |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| **Pre-existing disease (PED) waiting** | You can't claim PED-related until this passes | **Shorter (2–3 yr) beats 4 yr** |
| **Specific-disease waiting** | Cataract, hernia, etc. have their own waiting | Shorter |
| **Co-pay** | You pay a % of every claim | **Zero co-pay preferred** (esp. for individual under 60) |
| **Sub-limits / disease capping** | Caps on specific procedures | Fewer/none preferred |
| **Deductible** | Amount you pay before cover kicks in | Zero on base plan |
| **Permanent exclusions** | Never covered | Understand the list |

### 4. Cost (matters, but not first)
| Metric | Why it matters |
|--------|---------------|
| **Premium at current age** | Entry cost |
| **Age-band loading curve** | Premium at 45/55/65 — the real lifetime cost |
| **Premium revision history** | How often & how much they hike existing customers |

> Lowest premium often hides room-rent caps, co-pay, or sub-limits. Compare on **value per rupee**, not price.

### 5. Insurer Strength & Fairness
| Metric | Why it matters | Threshold |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **Solvency ratio** | Ability to pay claims | IRDAI minimum **1.5**; higher is safer |
| **Years in business / promoter** | Longevity & backing | Prefer established |
| **Grievance disposal** | Do they resolve complaints? | Higher disposal % |

### 6. Fine Print / Policy Longevity
| Metric | Why it matters |
|--------|---------------|
| **Moratorium period** | After 5 yrs (IRDAI norm) no claim can be denied for non-disclosure except fraud — shorter/clearer is safer |
| **Lifelong renewability** | Non-negotiable — must renew for life |
| **Claim-based loading** | Some insurers raise your premium after a claim — avoid |
| **Product revision/withdrawal history** | Withdrawn products force migration — check stability |
| **Free-look & portability terms** | Exit/switch safety |
| **Grace period** | Days allowed to pay a late renewal without losing continuity/waiting-period credit — longer (30 days) is safer |
| **Entry max-age limit** | Age beyond which you can't *first* buy the plan — distinct from lifelong renewal of an existing policy |

## Network Quality — measure density, not raw count *(from network image)*
Raw "network hospital count" is easy to inflate and geographically lumpy. A better metric is **hospitals per district** (÷ ~780 districts):

| Insurer | Network | Per district |
|---------|--------|:---:|
| HDFC ERGO | 13,600+ | ≈17.4 |
| Aditya Birla | 12,300+ | ≈15.8 |
| Bajaj Allianz | 12,200+ | ≈15.6 |
| Acko | 11,500+ | ≈14.7 |
| Royal Sundaram | 10,000+ | ≈12.8 |

> These deduplicated counts are **lower and better-verified** than the raw brochure figures used in Stage 2. What ultimately matters is **whether your city's top hospitals are in-network** — confirm per-plan in Module 3.

## What we deliberately down-weight
- **Freebies / OPD / wellness points** — nice-to-have, rarely decide a claim outcome.
- **Brand/advertising** — irrelevant to wording.
- **Agent recommendation** — conflicted by commission.
