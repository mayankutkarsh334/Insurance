# 🏥 Health Insurance Deep-Study

A structured, **data-driven** search for the best **individual health insurance policy** in India — one that actually *pays claims*, has minimal fine-print traps, and stays renewable for life at a fair premium.

No agent tips. No "Best Plan 2026" listicles. Every claim in this repo is traced to a **binding policy wording**, an **IRDAI disclosure**, or a **rating-agency report** — and where the marketing brochure and the legal wording disagree, **the wording wins and the gap is recorded as a finding.**

> ⚠️ **Not financial advice.** This is personal research tuned to one buyer's profile (below). Your right plan depends on your age, health, city and budget. Verify every number against the current policy wording before buying.

---

## 👤 Whom this is tuned for

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Buyer | **Individual**, age **26**, single life (own sum insured) |
| Location | **Metro tier-1** (IRDAI "Zone A") |
| Horizon | **Lifelong** — buy once, renew every year, never lapse |
| Sum insured studied | **₹10L · ₹25L · ₹50L · ₹1Cr** (how cover & cost scale) |
| Priority order | **Claims reliability › Coverage › Exclusions › Cost** |

> Health insurance is bought once and renewed for decades. The variable that matters most is **whether they pay when you claim** — not the lowest premium.

## 🔬 Method

```
   ~25 insurers / 50–70 plans
            │
            ▼  Stage 1 — hard filters (deal-breakers)
        ~10 plans
            │
            ▼  Stage 2 — comparative scoring
        5 FINALISTS  (+2 added later — see below)
            │
            ▼  6-module deep study each (benchmark-first)
   Weighted score /5  +  Module-6 override veto
            │
            ▼
        Ranked decision
```

### The 6 modules & weights
| Module | Weight | What it answers |
|--------|:------:|-----------------|
| **M3 · Claims & servicing** | **25%** | Will they actually pay — fast, in full? (ICR, complaints, TAT, network) |
| **M1 · Coverage & benefits** | **25%** | What you're really buying (room cap, bonus, restore, consumables) |
| **M2 · Exclusions & waiting** | **20%** | Where denials come from (PED/waiting, co-pay, sub-limits) |
| **M4 · Cost & premium** | **15%** | Price now *and* over a lifetime (age-curve, repricing risk) |
| **M5 · Insurer quality** | **10%** | Will the company survive & stay fair for decades? (solvency, rating) |
| **M6 · Fine print & longevity** | **5%** | Landmines in the contract — **carries an override veto** |

> **Override veto:** any Module-6 deal-breaker (no lifelong renewal, claim-based loading, chronic product withdrawal) disqualifies a plan **regardless of its weighted score**. **No plan studied triggered it.**

## 🔎 Two plans that were nearly missed

The study's own network-density evidence ranked **Acko 4th** and **Royal Sundaram 5th** nationally — yet **neither had ever been run through Stage 1.** Both were screened late:

- **Royal Sundaram Lifeline** — ✅ **passes** every hard filter (solvency 2.35×, health ICR 83.36%, no room cap, Elite PED 24mo). But at **~21/30** it ranks below every finalist: restoration covers **unrelated illness only** unless you pay extra, and it has the **thinnest network of the density leaders (≈12.8/district)**. **No deep study warranted.**
- **ACKO Platinum** — ❌ **eliminated on filter F3 alone** (ICR 57.82%, under the 60% floor) — and that elimination **exposed a flaw in the framework**. Acko passes every other filter, several best-in-study, and has **complaints of 15.58 per 10k claims against finalist Care Supreme's 42.00**. *A 6.7-point ICR difference eliminated the plan with the better claims experience, while the plan with the study's worst complaint ratio became a finalist.* Acko was promoted to a full deep study by explicit decision; the result (3.325) confirms the service edge is real but the contract is weaker.

> **Framework fix applied:** **ICR is never to be used as a standalone filter again** — it must be cross-checked against complaint ratio, CSR and settlement speed, and benchmarked against the insurer's **own segment** (SAHI ≈68% vs private general ≈77.5% vs PSU ≈97.3%), not the blended industry average.

## 🧭 Source-of-truth philosophy

| Source | Gives | Trust |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Policy wording** (PDF) | The legally binding truth — benefits, exclusions, waiting, sub-limits | **Authoritative — always beats brochure/ads** |
| Brochure / prospectus | Marketing summary | Directional; verify against wording |
| IRDAI Annual Report / Bima Bharosa | ICR, solvency, complaints, grievance data | Authoritative (insurer-level, not plan-level) |
| Insurer's **audited annual report** | Solvency, combined ratio, segment results | Authoritative — **use the licensed entity, not the group** |
| Rating agencies (ICRA/CRISIL/India Ratings) | Financial strength, solvency | Reliable — **read the rationale, not a headline** |
| Quote engine / aggregators | Live premium by age & SI, network size | Reliable for price; verify features in wording |

**Four rules the whole study follows:**
1. **A benefit only counts if it's in the wording.** If it's in the ad but not the contract, it does not exist.
2. **Prefer ICR + complaint ratio + repudiation rate** over headline "Claim Settlement Ratio" — CSR counts a rejected ₹5k claim the same as a paid ₹5L one.
3. **Never quote a premium or a rating from a text dump.** Rate charts and financial tables stack several grids per page and extract out of visual order — **render the PDF pages as images and read the figure in context.** *(This rule exists because a text-layer read overstated SBI's premiums by 16–32% across two modules before M4 caught it.)*
4. **Don't fabricate.** If a number can't be verified it is written as **"unverified"**, with the source that would confirm it named.
